Wheras the kinges highnes sendith ouer at this tyme mynding
to knoew the certaynte of all the ordenances and Municions at
Calais and the natures diuersites and goodnes od the same beyng
eyther in theching and custody of the ordinary master of the
ordinance there or in the extraordinary charge and keping extraordinary of
sir christofer Morris knyght the master of the kinges ordenance and how the same is vsed and bestowed sendith
ouer at this tyme the said sir christofer to Calais to make by the
advise and ouersight of therle of hertford his highnes commissioner there and the lord matravers
depute of the said towne & marches a parfait book of the same premisses
and to returne from thens with diligence / A lettere
was written from the said Counsaill to the sayd two
lordes desyring them to ayde and further helpe the said sir
christofor herin in such sorte as the kinges Maieste myght be
satisfied of his highnes desyre aforsaid accordinly /

A warrant was directed to sir Bryan Tuke knight
treasourer of the kinges chamber for the payment vn commaunding
hym to payve vnto sir christofer morres knight master of the
kinges ordenance aswell the somme repayring at this tyme
to Calais by the co by his highnes commaundement the some
aswell the somme of xvijli xijs sterling to be by hym repayd
to william Vxley Clerk of the ordenance and Henry Jonson
Sirveiour of the same for their Diettes by the space of
iiijxx viij dayes as also of the somme of xiijli vjs viijd for his
costes in this his said iournay and in recompence of his travailles
taken heretofore there at Calais int he pervsing and putting
in good ordre t of the ordenance there /

A lettere
Philippe de Bursalia flemyng siruant to Monsir de Bevers
and William Crake scotishman beyng in their Journey
by see in to toward scotland and with letteres out flaunders to the
kinges of Scottes and arryving at in Norfolk for
to repose them self wer at arrested by master Jerny
Jernyngham and sent to the courte the forsayd
Counsail perceyving vppon conference with the same
no cause why they shuld be stayed did not only dimisse
them incontinetly with their letteres vntouched but also
wrote by them aswel a lettere to the Chauncelour & others of
the pryvey counsail with the kinges of Scottes

pagebreak


declaring vnto the same that the stay of the said Philip
and William proceded rather for lack of discrecion of
suchas wer the Ministres of the say same then for
that it was the kinges pleasir they had any such
commaundement of the kinges Maieste so to do / asalso another
to my lord of Norfolk requyring hym to suffre the
take such ordre vppon the borders as they myght
passe in to scotland without any let or imprediment to
the contrary / in the which lettere also my lord of Norfolk
was aduertised that the kinges maieste had bene sumwhat
diseased with a tercian abut thankes be to god recouered
agayn to his Helthe / And for the better
conducyng of the sayd two staunfers thorowght
the realme eyther of them had a passporte
vnder in papaer only subscribed with the counsailes handes

Ragland & his wyef who had bene late siruantes with
the late Countesse of Sares and her sonne the
lord montague wer examined before the cownsail
tooching the burnyng of certain letteres after the
apprehension of the said lord Montague and beyng
fownd asfarforth as the counsail could at this present perceyve by
innocence of such matters as wer laied vnto them they wer
discharged & remitted home agayn /

Vppon complaynt made by the wardens of the latten fownder
in london that diuerse persones did convey in to the partes
of beyonde the sundry kyndes of Metall hauyng
no licence to the same

It was condestended and agreed by the counsail that the lord privey
seale and master Comptroller shuld write a lettere to sir Thomas
Palmer knyght porter of Calais and to signifie vnto hym
that they had bene suters to the kinges Maieste for his extraor
dinary anuite which he had at Calais according to his
desyre written vnto them in two severall lettees And
that having bycause they had not obteyned the same of the king
maieste that hauyng not such answer of the kinges Maieste
therin as they desyred they obteyned licence of
and the king that his owne presence here shuld conferre
moche to the obteynyng of his said sute they obteyned
licence for hym of the kinges maieste for vj wekes absence
which they had also signified to the kinges depuyre there
requyring hym thervpon to repaire hither as sone as he
myght for the sayd purpose /
