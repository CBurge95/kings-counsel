At Grafton the iijde day of September
being present /
The great Chamberlain of Englande
The great Admirall of England
The Bishopp of Duresme
The Treasorer of the kinges household
The Secretary Master Wriothesley
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Consultacion was had touching a defalcacion of Deputies
gunners and souldyours owt of those fortresses whereas
they maybe best spared

Richarde Wederell John Roylle Richard Anstey and John Griffyn
Tapsters that vagarauntly folowed the courte from place
to place and enh caused the pryce of victalles to be enhaunced
contraryto the kinges highnes proclamacions wer adiudged to be set
on the pillory in seuerall places the one at the courte an
other at Stratforde the iijde at Tossetre the iiijth at North
hampton and papers on their heddes declaring their offenses

Letteres wer brought from the Chauncelour of Englande
with the copye of sir John Wythringtons patent in
which he moved that the kinges pleasir might be
knowen whither he shuld make Denyzens after the
course of the chauncerie /

John Heron the bastard brother of Giles Heron was
brought by John Herd one of the porters before
the counsaill and eftsones examyned of his practise
of astronomye and necromancie sithens the prohibition
of the Late erle of Essex and aunswering indirectly
was remitted to warde again and chardged to put
in wrytting all his practise sithens the said prohibition
and the names of theim that haue requyred his
working in it and the names and dwelling places
of all such as he knoweth practisiuers of the said
craftes /

John aprice / gentleman put vp a bill of complaint against
Charles Fox of Ludlow in the countie of Salop gentleman
vpon a querelle of convenantes broken and thervpon
the saide Charles at the commaundement of the
Counsaill was warned by me the Clerk of the
Counsaill tappere before theim the next Daye
to make aunswer to the sayd bill /

Robert Swynerton of Swyneshed in the countye of Lincolbshire
was brought before the Counsaill by Thomas Marret
and John Burton of Rugbye in the county of warwyk
yemen which haue deposed vpon a booke that the said
Robert sayde at Rugby beforsaide the vj daye of this
present moneth thise woordes folowing /

O Jesus what a world is this that so many men
shall dye and all for one mans sake

Charles Fox appered before the Counsaill and after
