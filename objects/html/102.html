Wheras the Staplers haue made sute at sundry tymes
to the kinges highnes for to haue his graces grauntes renewed
touching their shipping of wolles / the tyme of which graunte
being made vnto them vndir his Maiestes priuey Seale is now
nere hand expired his Maieste of his moos gracyous goodnes
hath willed his counsaill aforsayd to delcare vnto George
vnto G Medley and Andrew Judde Staplers who v be
here forthe sayd sute in the name of all the cumpany
of Staplers / that his Maieste is contented and pleased
that the sayd Staplers shall haue licence vndir his
highnes pryvey Seale to shippe for oon yere now next
ensuyng beginnyng to accompt from the feast of the
Purificacion of our Lady in such forme and with such
condicions as is conteyned in their part made with the
kinges highnes for fyve yeres /

Letteres A lettere wer written to the Maiour of London to cause
the collectours of the Subsidy to forebeare the levyng
of such money of Albaro de asto dillo / Antonio Jais mes
Antonio de Mazuelo / Philip de aranda Straungers
as they wer cessed to pay to the king / vntill further
aduertisement of his Maiestes pleasir

A letteres was written to Richard Edgecombe and others
gyving them chardge eftsones to examyne the matyer
of the spoyle of the portugalles ship that came
on ground at plymmourg and tapprehende &
committe to prison the chief offendours therin /

A lettere vndir the Stamp was written to the
kinges Sollicitour and william Fermour to see Justice
procede in the tryall of certain felonyes Layed
to the chardge of Jone Harryson singlewoman And
further to examyn a certain ryot committed by diuerse
the siruantes of John Denton of Caverffeld against
John Harman gentlemanhusher and to certifie the same
to my Lord Chuancelour

John Baban Late siruant to the Lady of Salisbury
was examnyed before the counsail touching certain
matyers /

John Gough of London prynter was sent to the Flyt for paynting and
selling of sedycyous bookes

William Pett of Senock in the County of Kent was committed to the flytt
aswel for making an affray with sir Thomas Nevells syruantes as also
for that he was suspected to haue hunted in the for huntyng in parkes
in the county
