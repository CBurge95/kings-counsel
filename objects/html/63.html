Robert Sowthwell Esquier and William peter Doctor of
Lawe two of the kinges ordinary Counsaillours made
report of their procedinges in thexaminacion of the burnyng
matter tooching the burnyng of the stack of woodde in Surrey
and sayd that they had fownd who burned the furst stack
but it coud not yet be proved who had burned the fo last

Master Horsey & Master
Jasper Horsey Stuard & Wymonde Carew receyvour to
the Lady anne of Cleves wer before the Counsail to
know the kynges pleasir tooching her removing & other
thinges conteyned diuided in articles conteyned in a stedule
remaynyng with Master Wryothesley

John Barkeley of Canterbury browght appered before the counsail with
such witnesses as he had to depose tooching the mater
opened by hy of treason opened by hym at the
Moore to the kinges Counsail them beyng there
the xijth of October and the matter beyng herd
and appering false & to have beene promoted by the sayd berkley only of
Malice Doctor peter was discharged & the sayd
barkeley commaunded to pay vnto John Meere of the
towne of Cambridge bedel his costes whom he had
caused to be sent for a witnes his cos tenne shillinges
for his costes & one sir James piers w of Cambridge
aforsayd prist whiche vpon whose mowth vpon whose
report the said barklye grownded his accusacion was
committed to the colehowse /

George Whelpeley & Elys brooke wer examined of

Letteres wer written to the comptroller of Calais signifyng
vnto hym that the king highnes tooke in god parte his
serious & ernest proceding tooching thexecucion of his office
& that vpon his complaynt Letteres wer written vnto the
Richard a lee sirveiour of Calais to make answer vnto such
thinges as the sayd Comptroller in his said lettere (which
remayn with the Secretary Master Wryothesley) layd to
the surveiours charge / further commaunding the sayd
comptroller to stay all such fees as the superfluous
surveiours had there /

a Lettere sent to Richard a lee sirveiour of Calais to such
thinges as in a stedule inclosed in his lettere was sent vnto hym
with commaundement to vse hym self to the kinges chief officers
there as to his dewtie doth apperteyn

A Lettere vnto Thomas fowler of Calais declaring
the recept of a bill sent from hym conteynyng the
(as he thought) the extraordinary offices in Calais
and that in the Counsailles opinion he had sent a wrong
informacion vnto them and that therfor he shuld eftesones
prevse the said bill (the copy wherof whas sent vnto hym
trahed vpon sum poyntes) and thervpon vpon the sight cous
to send vnto them a perfait booke of the same thinges /

A Lettere of Justice vndre the stampe was sent to John Heron of chipchace
keper of Tyndale & Riddayesale / in the fauour of
at the sure of my lord of Norfolk /

the almayn at Calais and to cause the surveiour
