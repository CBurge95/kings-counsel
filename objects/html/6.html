At Ewelme the xxvjth daye of
August being present
The Lord great Chamberlain of England
The Lord great Admirall of England
The Bishopp of Duresme
The Master of the horse
Sir Thomas Wriothesley Secretary
Master Chauncellour of Thaugmentacions

A lettere was directed William Barington Sir Walter Stoner and
others for thexecucion of their Commission of Gaeole deliuerey of the
prison of Walingford

At Bicot the forsaide daye and the sayde
Counsaillours being present

John Heron the bastard brother of Giles Heron was sent to the
Counsaill from Sir Richard Long knight with a lettere to the Master
of the horse sumwhat the apprehension of him and of a
Sceptre and certain caractes in a plate touching coniucacions
founde in the house of the saide John Heron at the tyme of
his apprehension and aftre the redyng of the lettere and the deliuerey
of the same to the Master Wriotheseley Secretary it was agreed
by the Counsaill that the next daye folowing at Notley the
before mencyoned Lyons thenne in the custody of the Marshall
by the commaundement of the Counsaill shulde be brought before
theim at viiij of the clock and at ix of the clock that John
Heron should be brought before theim /

Lyone was examnyed touching the deposicions of Regnaldes and
Jone Newman wief of Hnery Newman of Harkney which
Depositions remayn with Master Wriothesley Secretary

A lettere was sent vndir the Stampe to the Lord Chauncellour
for the calling in again by a Supersideour of the Commission
for the Subsidie in the countie of Buckinghamshire / and the making
of a new Commission wherin commaundement was given him
to inserte Sir John Dauncy knight George Bulstred Robert
Cheyney Edmund Wyndesour Christopher Westcote and
John Goodwyn Esquiers /

John Heron the bastard brother of John Giles Heron was
examyned touching his knoweledge suspicion and consent
aswell of the treasons whereof his broher Giles was atteynted
as of any other and it could by no meanes by gathered by
any woord ot otherwise that he was fawty of the same
as appereth by his confession

Henry Heron an other brother of the saide Giles Heron being
examyned of the same pointes that his brethern wer made the
same aunswer that Christopher did in effect and yet he
was pressed vehemently bothe by hope and dispaire to haue
confessed /

A lettere was receiued from my Lord of Norffolk of the Daye
of August wherin he approued the deuise that John Heron
shuld haue the rule of Tyndale and Ryddesdale if the
kinges highnes could be soo contented Declaring in the
same lettere of the ueto agnes / his meting with the gentlemen of
the cuntrey and his hunting in Framyngham parke
And with the said lettere twoo other letteres subscribed with his
owne hand the one to syr Cuthbert Ratcliff the other to
John Heron concerning the practise for the submission of
the Tyndales vpon the which twoo letteres the Counsaill
debated and referred to the king the conclusion of the same
and thervpon went to the king to knowe his pleasyr /

A lettere was sent to Master Gostwyke for the diligent expodicion
of a warraunt directed vnto him for ixC xcvli iijli xiijs viijd
for the capitains porters gunners and souldyours wages in the
fortresses and bulwerkes
