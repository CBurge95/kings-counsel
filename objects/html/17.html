A warraunt vndir the stampe to sir Bryan Tuke knight
for the payment to william honnyng of xxs gyven to Master
Secretary Sadlers siruantes for the bringing vp of John
Heron Item xxs to the Sergeauntes of London for the bringing
of Lyens / Item xjli xiijs iiijd to the bishopp of Elphens
of Jrlande / Item xli to John Heron of Chipcase Item
vjli xiijs iiijd to Thomas Ogle / Item vjli xiijs iiijd to
Cuthbert Shasto by waye of the kinges rewarde

Jone Newman of Harkney and Reginaldes which wer
sent for before by a lettere to the shiriffes of London deposed
the same saynges of Lyons / before the Counsaill
touching the matier of the herons / which they
deposed before sir John Alen at London and no more
and lyons being brought face to face before J the said
Jone affirmed the same and none other /

John Heron and Heron which Master Sadler the kinges
Secretarie sent hither to Grafton being examyned of
the saide pointes aunswered in like forme / as their brethern
had doon before /

The Duke of Norffolk his letteres to the counsaill wer red
and alsoo an other enclosed in the same to be sent to
Onele / if the counsaill liked the contynue of them it
The lettere to the Counsaill was diuided in to foure partes
In the furst he diswaded peax to be taken with Oconor
onles the same woold put in sufficient pledges aswell
alsoo for the restitucion of such gooddes and catalles
as he and his had spoyled from the kinges Subgietes
The second parte requyred the counsailles furtheraunce
towardes the kinges Maiestie for thobteyneng of the
gift or sale of xijli a yere for sir William of Brereton
The thyrd parte perswaded that the keping of certain
casteles and fortresses in Jrland might be taken fro
the brother of Onele and delyvered
to the custody of
The Last moved punishement to be taken apon Oconor
the tooles and the Cavaualx in thexample of the rest
which letteres remayn in the keping of Master Wriothesley
Secretarye /
