A lettere was deuised and sent to John Fletcher off Rye
that whereas he hadd stayed iij flemmyshe shipps laden wyth
wynes comming owt off fraunce he sholde incontinently dismisse
them / constreyning them to break no bulcke nether to sell
any parte thereoff vnlesse they sholde wyllingly condestende
therevnto and hereoff hereafter in no cace to stay any
suche from being off themperowres subgectes from whens
so ever they sholde coom or wherewyth so ever s they shodle
be laden /

Letteres wer sent to Master Sadlair towching therle
off Casseilles and the sheref of Ayre to be agreed by his
good help and meane /

A lettere was written to the president off the marches off wales
etc towching a certeyne contention betwene the Erle off worcester
and the lorde ferrers abowght the mustering off meane to be
ordred by his discretion /

The condition off this recognisance is suche as if thabouebonden
Arnolde do restore and redeliuer to the kinges vse all suche
gowlde or other treasour as hauing ben taken owt off the
frencheprise in myldeforde hauen is coom in to his handes / or
shall hereafter by any meane coom in to his possession / and do
aduertise from tyme to tyme off all suche parsons as he shall
hire of know to haue in theyre possessions any of the saide gowlde
than etc. orells etc. /
