The Vicechambrelains of the kinges and quenes syde with
diuerse other gentlemen the kinges and the quenes siruantes
wer sent for vnto whom at their cummyng it was
declared and alsoo commaundement gyven vnto them
to declare the same vnto others / their felowes then absent
that the kinges pleasir was they shuld fromhensforth in
no wise / molest his personne / with any maner sute but to
put their sutes in writing and deliure the same to such of
his graces ordinary counsaill as was appoynyed to attende
vpon his Maiestyes person for those and like other
purposes which counsaill shuld take such ordre in their
said sutes from tyme to tyme as shall apperteyne /

The gromes of the kinges priuey chambre wer commanded
euery of them to sirue the kinges highnes in his owne person
in doing of such thinges in the said chambre as apperteyn
to their office and in no wies to suffre either the pages
or any other person to entremedle in the same / onles
it wer in case of gret necessitye

The Chambrelain and Comptroller of the princes household
wer before the counsaill according to the tenour of a lettere
sent vnto them in that behaulf

The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Sethe Holand prist chapelain vnto Master Pates the kinges
Ambassadour resident with themperour was examyned of a
lettere sent vnto him and his said Master from John / Hilyares
Late vicar of Eastmayn now abyding and being penitenciary
at Rome and being founde clere from any spot of the said
Hilyares faction was dischardged

Wheras Rogier Lee of yvingo in the countye of Buckinghamshire gentleman Edward
Bowland of London gentleman and Thomas Norton of London aforsaid grocer
being examnyed by the said counsaill of all such matiers of variaunce
as wer betwene theim concerning the leasse of a ferme or graunge
called pvlloxhill graunge frankely et freely surrendred and
submitted to the ordre of the said counsaill all such right
title and interest as they or any of theim haue had or pretended
to haue to the sayd graunge by any maner meanes It is there
vpon determined adiudged and Decreed by the said counsaill
that the said Rogier Lee shall haue occupye and kepe still
the sayd graunge in such maner and forme as is specified in a
leasse made vnto him therof by the kinges generall Surveyours
vnder the gret seale of England and shall further paye for
the same / vnto the said Edwarde Bowland or his assignes /
yerely during such tyme as he the said bowlande / hath any
terme of yeres in the sayde graunge / xiiijli sterling at the feastes
of Saincte Michaell tharchaungell and thannunciation of our
Lady last past by evyn porcions except the Last yere of the
sayd bowlandes terme at the beginning of the which yere
or within six wekes next after the beginning of the same he
shall paye vnto the sayde bowlande / or to his assignes xxli
sterling in full contentacion and payment of all sommes of money
due by the said Lee to the sayd Bowland for any interest
he hath in the said graunge / It is alsoo further decreed
that a certain pretensed bargain betwene the forsaid bowland
and Norton touching the said graunge was and is of none
effect or force and that therfor the said Bowland or his
assignes shall deliure again and paye within the space of
a moneth now next ensuyng vnto the said Norton or his
assignes the somme of fourescore poundes which he receiued of
the sayd Norton for the sayd graunge And shall alsoo
suffre the sayde Rogier Lee peasibly and quietely to enioye
the sayde ferme or graunge without making any claime / sute
impedyment / or other maner let by himself or any other
by his meanes / to or for the saide graunge / And further

it is adiudged and decreed that forasmuche as the said Thomas
Norton went about vndir the colour of a false suggestion
to deceyve the kinges Maieste and for the better compassing
of the same / gave vnto philip van vilders who knew
not of his false entent one hundred poundes sterling he
the said Norton shall forfayte and loose without all
recouerey vnto the said Philip the said somme the of Cli
which the said Philip receiued of him by waye of rewarde
And furthermoore it is decreed that the said Norton
shall not by himself or any other personne by his meanes
vndir the colour of any bargain heretofore made by or
with him for the same maee any maner claime or title to
the said ferms or graunge not shall trouble sue or inquiet
the said Rogier Lee Philip Van Vilders or Edward Bowland
or any of theim for or by vertue of any bargain or maner
of acte or thing heretofore doon or mayde by the saide
Edward Rogier Philip or any of them with him the said
Norton for or concerning the said graunge but clerely
release acquyte and discharge them and euery of them
of all maner accions sutes querell & demaundes that
he hath or canne haue / against them or any of them
for the same / And finally it is adiudged & decreed
that the said Thomas Norton shall at sum tyme befor
the feast of Saint Andrew thapostle now next
ensuyng bring and deliuer vnto the handes of one of
the said Counsaillours / a leasse which he hath
vndir the kinges gret seale of England of the said graunge
to be cancelled / and shall alsoo deliure before the
said feast vnto the said Rogier Lee / or his assignes
all such other writtinges escriptes or munymentes of what
soeuer nature they be as he hath of or concernynyg
the said ferme or graunge without any maner fraud
gile or craft to be by him wrought contrary to the
meaning of this decree /

The condicion of this recognisaunce is that if the said Rogier
Lee doo obserue fulfyll and kepe all and singuler such articles
clauses decrees and determinacions comprised in the decree
aboue written which on his parte ar to be obsirued & kept
that thenne etc.

It is agreed that a certain felowe remayning in the porters
warde as suspect of the robberye of a Straunger vnto
whom he was guyde shalbe sent furthwith vnto the Marshalsye
there to remayn vpon a further examinacion

Letteres wer sent to the Surveyour of the workes at Dover
to repayre to the kinges highnes if he might without daungier
of his helth and to bring with him the plattes of the workes
there and also borough Maye and Conchy orels if he could
not convenyently cum himself to sende the said plattes by the
said personnes and to enforme theim of his hole deuise / touching
the same /

The condicion of this Recognisaunce is that if the forsaid John
Butler doo absteyn at all tyme and tymes from taking or
killing in any maner place within the kinges Realme of England
any partriches and fesantes without any nettes setters horses traneles
or other gynnes that then etc. /

The condicion of this recognisaunce is that if the foresaid william
white doo absteyn at all tyme and tymes from taking or
killing in any maner place within the kinges realme of England
and any partriches fesantes with any nettes setters horses
traneles or other gynnes that then etc. /

Letteres wer sent to the Deane of wyndesour Master chamber and
the Mayour of the said Towne to enquyre what houses
be infecte in Wyndesour and to cause the inhabitauntes
of the infect houses with their families and household
stuff to avoyde the towne / to sum place of good distaunce
from thens and from such other places as where the kinges
highnes resorteth Signifying vnto them that the kinges
highnes wold beare the charges of their removing /

John Storye put in a bill of complaint and a certain
other bill conteyning articles of treason against Davye
Gunston and being herd what he could say for the prove
of the same / forasmuch as his proves semed vncertain
and to depende much vpon the sainges of one Philip Babington
who at this tyme is not nere to be spoken withall was commaunded
vpon payn of his allegiaunce to be here before the counsaill
vpon sonday being the xvijth of this present moneth and to
bring with him the said Philip Babington and in the meane
tyme to disclose this complaint to no person
