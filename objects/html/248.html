At Hull the xijth day od Septembre
being the same presence of the day
before

Richard De Saul of Hull Marchaunt complayned vnto the lordes
and others of the Counsail that wheras in the commociin tyme he
deliured by the commaundement of the Maiour at that tyme certain
municion of warre / the same wer never deliured vnto him again
beseching them to call before them the Maiour and thaldermen of
the said towne and in consideracion of the true and loyall
syrvice which he thenne did to the kinges Maieste to cause
recompence to be made vnto him / according to reason whervpon
the said Maiour and Aldermen appering before the said Counsail
after debatement of the matier wer commaunded to content &
pay furthwith vnto the said Richard Saull such a somme of money
in recompence of the sayde municion as he wold swere thee
same wer worth /

A lettere was written to syr Cuthbert Ratclif knight
deputy wardeyn of the middel marches foraccempst Scotland
that whereas enformacion was gyven vnto the forsaide Counsail that John Heron of Haulbarnes being was of long tyme deteyned
in prison at Newcastel vpon the surmise of Marche treason
and that in the meason two warden courtes being passed / there had
bene nothing proved against him A lettere was written to syr
Cuthbert Ratclife knight deputy warden of the middell marches
foraccempst Scotland in cace the said enformacion wer
true to put the sayd heron to baile vppon sufficient suerties
to be furthcumming at all tymes to answer vnto such thinges
as shalled layed to his charge accordingly

Wheras the kinges Maieste was being credibly enfourmed that the Mayour / and
Cytyzens of london had determined to chose Master Denam one of
thaldermen there to be their Mayour for the next yere now ensuyng
and consydering that the sayd denam by reason of his enfirmity
and age shuld not able to supply the rowme as apperteyned /
A lettere was written by the said Counsaill vnto the lordes and others
of the kinges Maiestes counsail at london to requyre whither the
sayde Cyty entended the said elecytion or no / and in cace they
perceyved the same to be true / to sende incontinently for the Maiour
Recourdour and such other of thaldremen their as they shuld thinke mete
