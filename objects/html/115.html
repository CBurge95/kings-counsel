A lettere was sent to syr Richard Southwell knight to sende vp
to london with all convenyent diligens from Alyngton castle all
the plate and such household stuff as he by his discrecion shuld
think mete for the kinges Maiestes vse togidir withall the fuff of
the Armory gunnes Jeuelles and gret horses and alsoo to dischardge
fromthens the Lady poyninges and yong wyattes wief and also
to enquyre of Masteres Darrell whither she entended to go to any
such place wheras she shuld be ordered as that wherwithall
she appered to be might be presirued and in cace she woold not
declare vnto him the same wherby he might coniecture that
might perishe which she had conceyved then to stay her there
vntil the kinges highnes further pleasyr wer knowen therin
And furthermore calling before him all wyattes syruantes
and gyving them a good lesson to vse themselfes honestly
to gyve vnto euery of them his halfe yeres wages by way
of his Maiestes moost gracyous rewarde for the furniture
wherof if he lacked of his owne store then he shuld make
shift their for money which at is retourne shuld be payed
vnto him again / And thiese thinges doon to delyvre the
house and all other thinges which he shuld leave behynde him
by Inventory to the custody of william Culpeper to be
savely kept by him vntill he shuld have further knoweledge
of the kinges Maiestes pleasyr in that behaulf

The Condicion of this recognisaunce is such that if the
abouebounden syr Oswald wolstrop doo personally appere befor
syr Thomas Audeley knight Lord Audeley of Walden Chauncelour
of Englande and other the lordes of the kinges moost honorable counsail
Chamber in the Starre chambre at Westminster within a moneth next after the
feast of Easter next cummyng to aunswer vnto such thinges as shalbe

obiected vnto him and so doo appere from Daye to Daye vntil
such tyme as he shalbe dischardged in that behawlf that then
etc. orels etc. /

Wheras John Augustyne of Buckfastlee in the Countye of Devon
aylmer in the name of thinhabitantes of Buckfastlee fynfeld and
other the princes tenantes being borderers to the Forest of Dartmore
did exhibite vnto the kinges Maieste a bill of complainte against
syr Thomas Denys knight shewing in the same that the sayd syr
Thomas did not only withholde wrongfully from theim such right
and vse as they clayme to haue and as they saye they haue had
tyme out of mynde in and to the commyn of the moores called
holmore Buckfastle More Brent Moore to the great preiudice
of the kinges Maiestes and the princes graces right in the sonne but
alsoo that the syruantes of the saide syr Thomas doo withholde and kept
from theim aswel the free digging and carying awaye of Marle
in a put called / the quarye of the manour of buckfastley as alsoo forbidde
theim the vse of a payer of buttes and of a pathe waye to their
parishe churche of the said towne of Buckfastlee to the great losse
Detrimient and vtter vndoing of the said inhabitantes to the which
bill of complaint the said syr the said Thomas hath made aunswer
by month and bene herd saye at good length what he could alledge
forhimself to the contrary The kinges Maiestes Counsaill aforsaid
pondering and considering indifferently with an upright iudgement
the deposicions and allegacions of bothe partyes and perceuing none
other yet by any thing that hath bene hitherto alledged but that the said
inhabitantes and tenantes haue vsed and had in the Late Abbottes of the
Late Abbot Monk of Buckfast tymes such right interest and vse as they
clayme to haue in & to the said marle put buttes chuchwaye and commyn
in the forsaide Moores / doo ordeyn Judge and Decree that aswell
the said inhabitantes of Buckfastlee shal have vse and occupye freely
the buttes standyng in the browne parke in Buckfastley the patheway

pagebreak


to the church lying from Kylbery agaynst harpsbridge vnto the
churche of buckfastley / & Digge Marle in the said pit and caryawaye
the same feely at their liberty and in such wise as they did of
haue and occupye the said waye and buttes and digge and caryawaye
the said Marle in the Late Abbot of Buckfast his tyme / as alsoo that
the said inhabitantes of Buckfastlee / Fynfeld and all other
the princes tenantes borderes vpon the forest of Dartmore / shal
enioye and kepe still all such commyn pasture and other right
as they had occupied and vsed in the said Late Abbotes tyme
without paying any other sommes of money or being otherwise vsed
for the Dryving or poundyng of their catell thenne they wer
wont to pay or be vsed in the said late Abbotes dayes without the
let trouble or contradiction of the said syr Thomas or his siruantes
or of any other personne or persones by his commaundement procurement
or meanes / vntil such tyme as vppon certificate to be made from
certain commissioners / appointed to examyn the right of both
the says parttes the Counsaill shall otherwise ordre and
determyne in this behaulf /

Wheras Gyles Ash Hasbard of the towne Southampton
berebruer had laden in a shippe of holland named
the Marye of Durdroyt fyve pockettes of wolle
mynding to haue conveyd the same in to Flaunders
without any licence obtenyed for that behaulf the same contrary to
diuerse actes heretofore for in that behaulf After
the which fact was duely & manifestly proved before
John Mille of Southampton aforsayd and John White
of Sowthwike Esquiers and William Shurland gentleman
Capitayn of the kinges Castel at Calshore poynt
and certificat made therof certificat vndir theyr seales vnto the Co Kinges Maiestes
Counsail aforsayd After mature deliberacion and conference
had with the kinges lerned Counsail for their opinions
& advises tooching the ordre & determinacion of the laws
in the forsayd case It is was determined iudged & decreed
& the same decree aduertised to the forsayd Comissionours
to thentent they shuld put the same in execucion that
the sayd berebruier be according to certain actes made
in anno viij henrici vjti Ca xvij & xix the sayd berebruer
be apprehended & committed toward there to reioy be
furthcummyng to answer to such informacion as shuld be put
in against him to thexchequir in that behaulf and
that the woolles & all such other gooddes as be the
owner Masters of the shippe shuld be praysed & sted
to the kinges vse / and furthermore forasmoche as thone
half of the ship belonged to the master the ship shuld be
indifferently praysed & the sayd Master & so suffred to
passe with her way with all such other gooddes in her as wer not
forfait saving that and that nevertheles the sayd Master be
the value of the halfdele of the ship beyng his parte after
the rate as she wer promised / And further it was
decreed that it the sayd Comissionours shuld deduct
of the sayd money to be raysed of the sayd forfayctes such
somes as they had defrayd in sending hither to & for prosecuting the sayd
matter
