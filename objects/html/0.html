At Wyndesour the xvij day of
August being present
The Lord pryvey Seale
The Lord great Chamberlain of England
Therle of hertforde
The Bishopp of Duresme
The Bishopp of Winchestre
Master Comptroller
Master ViceChambrelain
Master Secretary
Master Chauncellour of the tenthes etc /

A lettere was written to syr Bryan Tuke knight Treasorer of the kinges
chambre to see Thomas Fowler of Calais and six or eight
sufficient Suertyes with him bounden in a recognisaunce before a Master
of the Chauncerye in tenne thousandes markes aswell to make thaccompte
of Robert Fowler his brother decessed Late vicetreasorer of Calais
as alsoo to paye such sommes of money as it shall appere vpon the
Detormynacion of the same accompte that the sayde Robert at the
tyme of his Death stoodde indebted to the kinges highnes Whensoeuer
the sayde Thomas shalbe called vpon for the same /

The forsayde Daye a lettere was receyued from the Deputye and
Counsaill of Calais bearing Date the xth daye of August wherin
sute was made for certain reparacions to be Doon out of hand at
Calais vpon the Sluse the paradyse and the filling of the West
Jettys And further making mention of a Declaracion sent alsoo
at this tyme in a Scedule aparte what nombre of shippes resorte
yerely ato Calais and of the ancorage of the same / and how long
the bringyng of Stones for balasse hath been lefte /

It was afreed that a priuey Seale shuld be sent to William Barker /
to appeare before the Counsaill wheresoeuer the same wer vpon
payn of his allegeaunce to aunswer wherfor he had not accomplished
the Decree made by the Counsaill touching the matier of his
Doughter Anne Barker accoring to their commaundement declared
vnto him in a lettere sent vnto him for that purpose /

John heron keper of Tyndale was moved by the Counsaill on
the kinges behaulf for the keping still of Tyndale with an offre of

pagebreak


the howse and the demaynes of Wrythryngton for an augmen
tacion of his entreteynement without paying any rent for the
same during the tyme of his contynue in the sayde office
and answered resolutely that he was not able to serve the
king in that office as shulde apperteyn onlee he might also
haue the keping of Ryddesdale /

A lettere was written to the Deputye of and Counsaill of Calais
signifyng to the same the receipte of their letteres and other writtinges
and that the kinges highnes for the present reparacions which they
requyred to be doon had caused his pleasour to be signified to
the Surveyour there mynding nevertheles vpon deliberacion
to signifie vnto theim herafter moore at Large an aunswer
touching all the requestes of their sayd letteres

A lettere written to the Duke of Norffolk wherin was briefely
Declared vnto him the procedinges of the Bishopp of Bathe
with the Duke of Cleve / and also shewing to him the kinges
pleasure touching the cummyng vpp of the Tyndalles with
halters about their neckes

After none appered before theim Edmund Peckham Esquier
Cofferar of the kinges household for the redresse of certain
matiers touching the ordre of the kinges sayde household
as for the lodging of the mullettes of the cariage and for
the cariage of the Lady Margaretes stuff

Vpon occasion of a bill that was put vp to the king the xxj of
this present moneth at his entre in to Reding by the Mayer &
Burgesse of the same William Pennyson and Vachell
the kinges officer wer sent for the declare what libertyes
the towne had in Thabbotes dayes / touching the Mattier
proponed in the Maiours supplicacion and after their deposition
cam before the sayd Counsaill Justice that tyme
Mayour and thre other burgesses / which affirmed theyr
election of the yere passed to haue bene by the Late Erle
of Essex appoyntement / And for an other parte of the
Maiours sute which was for a falling Milbe aunswer
was made that they shuld haue one and that the platt
thereof shuld be at the appoyntement of the kinges officers
with theyr owne aduise to the same /

A lettere of Justice was directed to the Deputye of Calais
vpon the complaint of Thomas Combes of Guisnes against
Sir Thomas Palmer knight porter of Calais for the
withholding aswell of vjli xiijs iiijd debt to the sayd Combes
by a bill of Sir Thomas Palmers hand as alsoo of Cvj acres
Land which the sayd Combes claymeth in the right of
his Wief

A lettere was directed to the Maiour of Calais for the restitution of the
William Brysewood the younger vnto the Landes of his father decessed
which wer after the decesse of the sayd Brisewoode thelder

pagebreak


seassed to the kinges vse vpon an informacion made to the Counsaill
of Calais that dying surveiour of the kinges workes he stoode endebted
to the king in great sommes of money

The forsayde William Brisewood the younger was bounde in a
recognisaunce of CCli sterling the same being taken by the
Chauncellour of thaugmentacion / the condicion whereof was this
that in case a further debt canne be proued due by his father
to the kinges highnes / thenne is alredy payed and which his
movable gooddes be able to recompence if thenne he restore to
the kinges vse the sayde Landes and the profetes of the same /
taken by him in the meane season and in this suspense do none
acte preiudiciall to the king in this behaulf thenne etc orels etc

Sir William Pitt prest Vicare of Bambery was commanded
vpon his alleageaunce to be the xxix Daye of this moneth
at Graston before the Counsaill

A lettere was written to the Lord Sandes the kinges Chamberlain
in the fauour of Thomas Combes of Guisnes who had put
vpp a bill of complaint against him before at Wyndesour

Certain men of Calais which for their offenses wer banished
the towne and after made sute to the kinges highnes that it
please his grace for that they had bene long in his syriuce
to appoynt theim to sum other wher they might be entreteined
had their aunswer by the mouthe of the Counsaill that the
king wold not medle with theim but that they must shaft
honestly for their lyving otherwise /
