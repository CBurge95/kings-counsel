At Hamptoncourte the xviijth of
Januarye being present
The Lord Chauncelour
The Duke of Norffolk
The Duke of Suffolk
The Lord priuey Seale
The great Chambrelain of Englande
Therle of hertforde
The Bisshopp of Duresme
The Treasorer of household
The Comptroler of household
The Master of the horses
The vicechambrelain
Sir Thomas Wryothesley Secretary

A lettere was sent to the Comptroller of Calais to allowe
in his bookes of comptrollement / vnto the Surveyour of the kinges workes there aswell
for his diett after the rate of iiijs by the day aswell for his
absence hitherto asfor all such other tyme as he shall soo
be absent herafter by the kinges his Maiestes commaundement and
like wise to allowe vnto him his full wages due and to be due
which vppon this and other thinges moved betwene them he
they sayd Comptroller had foreborned to allowe vnto the sayd
Surveyour

A lettere vndir the Stampe was sent vnto the Deane of Yorke
and others / that wheras the kinges Maieste being aduertised by their
letteres written vnto the Master of the horses and Master Secretary Wryothsly
of twoo women which had doon and committed detestable offences
in those partyes / his Maiestes pleasyr was that they shulld
furst cause the woman that burned the house to be endyted
of the sayde facte and theruppon to be arraigned and to se her
put thexecucion of such death as by the Law is limited & appointed
in that behaulf and for the punishement of thother woman to
cause her tongue to be perced and slitt through with a burnyng
Yron to thintent she shall neuer after disclose her vicious
and abhominable doinges

A lettere to John Mills of Southhampton White of Southwik
and to ye Capitain of Calsher point to stay a ship that had receiued
wolles aboorde without licence or paying of custume and
aswell to take an inventory of all such thinges as wer in the
shippe as also to cause the maister & owner of the same to be
furth cummyng & thervpon to aduertise the Counsail of their
proceding /


At Hamptoncourte the xixth daye of
Januarye being present the same
Counsailours that wer the day befoor

A lettere was sent to Fraunces Sidney to dischardge
Masons wief & her siruantes and to commaunde her with her syruantes to
returne to London to her howse /

A lettere was sent to syr Thomas Nevill and syr
Richard Southwell knightes commaundyng them to
sende certain coffers of Masons which wer brought
to Alington Castell to be returned back to London
there to be syrched by Robert Southwell Esquyer
whom the kinges highnes had appointed for the same
purpose /

Two seueral letteres of oon effect wer sent to tharchebishops
of Canterbury and York requyring theim to cause a certain
booke of attestacions which was sent vnto either of theim
the oon by hopkyns and the other by John the
concernyng the matyer betwene the kinges Maieste and the Lady Anne
of Cleves to be not only entred in their registre booke of that
yere when the thing was done by alsoo either of them to see the
book which was sent vnto them to be savely kept soo that it might
be furthcummyng /
