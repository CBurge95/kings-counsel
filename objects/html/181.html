Gyles Hasebarde of the Towne of Southhampton Berebruer
who had laden in a ship of hollande named the Mary of Dordroyte
fyve pocketes of wolle myndyng to haue conveyed the same
in to flaundres / likas it appereth the xxxth day of January
Last past without any licence obteyned for the same contrary
to diuerse actes made heretofore in that behalf was brought
before the Counsaill and for his punishement was committed
to the Flete / therin to remayne during the kinges pleasyr /


At Grenewiche the xxvth of april
beyng present the Counsail which was
present the daye before /

It was agreed that Antony Marler of london marchant / might sell the
bibles of the gret volume vnbounde for xs sterling and bounde
being trymmed with bullyons for xiijs sterling /
