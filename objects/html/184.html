At Grenewich the xxixth day
of Aprill being present
The Duke of Norffolk
The Lord pryvey Seale
The gret Chambrelain of Englande
The Erle of Hertforde
The bisshop of Duresme
The Treasorer of household
The Comptroller of household
Sir Thomas Wriothesley Secretary
Sir Rauf Sadleir Secretary

It was agreed that Morgan Welles and John Blackwell
yeoman syruantes to Master Paston one of the gentleman of the
priuey Chamber shuld be dischardged of their
recognisance wherin they wer bonde at westminster the
xviijth of Marche / in the xxxijth yere of the kinges
highnes Reign /

A lettere vndir the stampe was written to the lord Matravers
Deputy of Calais requyring him notwithstandyng a former lettere
written from the kinges highnes to the contrary requyring him
to suffre the Comptroller of Sir Edward Ringeley Comptroler
of Calais to enioye still the house which he had of the
lady Garnishe / notwithstandyng a former lettere written vnto
him the said dedputy from the kinges highnes in the fauour
of Anton Knevet chief Porter of Calais to the contrary and that in lieu
of the said house housl the said Deputy shuld deliuer
vnto the said Portre the house wherin syr Thomas
Palmer Lately dwelled /

A lettere vndir the Stampe was written to Antony Rous
Treasirour of the workes at Gusnes / to content and pay vnto
Richard Musgrave and one gilton bearer vndi and lx horsmen
prouided in the North their wages from the xjth day of
May next ensuyng that is to say to the said Musgrave
being a peticapitain ijs mere sterling / by the day to the
gilton bearer xijd by the day and to euery horseman
ixd sterling by the day as also like wages vnto the b the other bande
of h xl horsemen provided by Master Wallopp / their peticapitan
and gilton bearer / begining taccompt the furst of May
next if they wer all then redy or els at such tyme to
so many as shalbe then redy the residue to be payed
as they be admitted & ministred /
