At Windesour the xvjth of
Novembre being present
The Duke of Suffolk
The Lord priuey Seale
The gret admiral of England
The Bishopp of Duresme
The Lord Chambrelain to the king
The Comptroler of household
The vicechambrelain
Sir Thomas Wriothesley knight Secretary

Thomas Thwaytes was sent to the towre of London by certain
of the garde with a lettere to the Lieutenant declaring his confession
and commaundyng hym that in cace he woold stande stil in
denyal to showe of whom he had herd the thinges he confessed
he shuld gyve him stretche or twoo at his discrecion vpon
the brake

A lettere to sir Thomas Arundel to send vpp Jone Benbury
which was accused of Trayson and had confessed parte of the same

A lettere to therle of hertford to send to the Counsail the booke of
the description of j men appoynted to haue gone over at such
tyme as the sayd Erle was sent to Calais

A lettere to the Deputye of Calais to appoint Fraunces Haull
in the absence of the Surveiour to wyne with stephen thalmain
for the making of the platt of the Marches and passage
about Cowbridge / vntil such tyme as the Suveyour wer
sent home again

A lettere vndir the Stampe to the Treasorer & Comptroler
of Calais for the payment of Thomas aboroughes wages
for the tyme of his absence in the kinges siruice notwithstanding
any Statute to the contrary

Thomas Derby the Quenes Secretary put vp a bill of
complaynt against sir Edward Willoughby wherupon it
was decreed that a writt vndir the pryvey Seale shuld
be sent vnto the said sir Edward tappere before the
Counsail vpon the sight therof


pagebreak


At the sute of Tho Dauid Vincent of the wardrobe of the kinges
beddes / three seuerall pryvey Seales wer sent for Miles Forest
Robert Browne and to appere before the
Counsail vpon the sight thereof

Richard Ledes was examyned before the Counsail as suspect of
pyracy and standing in the denyal of such matyer as was Layd
to his chardge was committed to warde /
