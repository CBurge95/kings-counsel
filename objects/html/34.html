At Hampthill the xxvth of September
being present /
The Lord gret Chamberlain of England
The gret Admyrall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Treasorer of household
The Master of the horses
The Secretary Master Wryothesly
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Letteres wer sent to my Lord pryvey Seale / for the
delyverey of sir Geffray Poole / with condycion that he
shuld agree with the party and also in nowise approche near
to the kinges presence nor cum to the courte vntill the
his highnes pleasir wer further knowen in that behaulf


At Hampthill the xxvj of Septembre
being present
The Lord gret Chambrelain of England
The gret Admyrall of England
The Bishopp of Duresme
The Treasorer of householde
The Master of the horses
The Secretarye Master Wriothesley
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Thomas Smyth of Bedforde was brought before the Counsaill
by Hughe Willoughby Sergeaunt at Armes and was examined
before the Cousnaill of the affraye made betwene him
Gybbes and William Olyver yomen of the garde

Thomas Fulgha of Ryseley appered before the Counsaill
and delyuered in wrying an answer to Thomas Huettes
complaynt

Letteres wer sent from the Counsaill to the Custumers &
Comptroller of the kinges porte of London to certifie what
custume or subsydy was due for leader / vpon the occasion of
a complaynt made vnto the Counsail by the frenche ambas
sadour agaginst the sircheour of london /


pagebreak


Letteres wer sent vndir the stampe to the bailliffes of Saint
Albons / for the sendyng vp hith to the courte a certain
fellow taken as suspecyous of the robbery of a Straunger
vnto whom he was guyde /


At Ampthill the xxvijth of September
being present
The gret Chambrelain of England
The gret Admyrall of England
The Bishopp of Duresme
The Treasorer of household
The Master of the horses
The Secretary Master Wriothesley
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Thomas Fulgham of Rysely was comytted to the porters
warde by the Counsailes Commaundement there to remayn
vntill he founde sufficient suretyes for the peax

The forsayde Thomas Smyth after the examynacion of the
affray betwene him & two yemen & Gibbes &
Olyver yemen of the garde / being not founde somuch
in faute as they wer / and having monition to vse thymselfes
honestly towardes the kinges siruantes was set at liberty /
