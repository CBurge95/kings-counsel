At Hamptoncourte the xviijth of
February being present
The gret ChamberLain of England
The Treasorer of the kinges household
The Master of the kinges horses
The vicechambrelain
Sir Thomas Wriothesley Secretary

Master Tuke was requiyred by a lettere eyther to
leave here a somme of money for the dispeche of postes
from tyme to tyme as they shuld be sent furth with the
kinges letteres orels tappoint one tattende here specially for
that purpose for otherwise the kinges highnes affayres
shuld be for Lack of money here in arredines gretly hindred


At Hamptoncourte the xixth of february
beyng b present
The lorde privey seale
The gret chamberlayn of England
The Gret Admyral of England
The Treasourer of the household
The Comptroller of household
The vicechamberlayn
Sir Thomas Wriothesley Secretary
The Chauncelour of the tenthes

A Lettere was sent to the president of the Counsail in Wales
requyring him not only to aduertise the kinges highnes of
the trouth and circumstance of sundry murdyer committed
there in the cuntrey by certain walshemen but also
to signifie to his Maieste what personnes they were
that had so offended and vppon what grounde and
in what facion they had committed the same and praying
him to vse diligence aswell in aduertisement thereof
as also how the cuntrey is disposed and inclined at
this present
