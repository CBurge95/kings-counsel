And wheras John Gunter william wayte and John Enely
of Chichestre Esquyres aduertised the Counsaill by ther
letteres / that accourding to the purport of a lettere directed vnto
them from the said Counsaill / that they had caused
the whete Laden in a certain flemishe hoye stayed their
by them to be brought on londe and sold the same
at vjs viijd the quarter / to be payed at the feast
of penthecost next ensuynge / A lettere was sent vnto
the said Gunter & Wayte wherby and Enely / requyring them
that forasmuch as there was appered a playn bargain betwene
the owners of the said hoye and one Bowyer of
Peteworth / for the sayd hoye and fourty quarters
of wheate And that for thaccomplishment therof
they brought the ship in to the haven there / they were requred to delyver
vnto the said Fleminges the said hoy and all other
thinges conteyned in the same / and allso to take such
ordir with them as the money for xl ty wuarters of wheate
after the rate a they had sold ithe same / might be
satisfied at their appointement at the Dayes agreed
for the payment therof / and wheras there was laden
in the said hoye xviij quarters of wheat more then
was entred in the cocket of the Custumers of Southhampton
forasmuche as the same wes plainly forfaited by the
kinges maiestes Lawes / they wer requyred to receyve the moy
to be made of the said wheate therof and deducting the chardges for
binging it on Lande after the rate of the quantity
to convert the one half to his Maiestes vse & the other haulf
to such a wer the Seysons therof of the same
