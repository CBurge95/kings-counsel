At Buckyngham the xxviij
of August being present
The great Chamberlain of England
The great Admirall of England
The Bishopp of Duresme
The Master of the horse
Sir Thomas Wriothesley knight Secretary
The Chauncellour of Thaugmentacion

A lettere was sent to the warden of the Flit to receiue in to
his custodye Christopher Heron and Henry Heron and to kepe
theim a parte gyving theim sufficient meate and Lodging
at the kinges reasonable charges

Lyone was committed to the custodye of the knight marshall
folowing the courte

John Heron the bastard was committed to the porters Warde
to remayn all four in the forsaide wardes vntill the cummyng
of twoo other Brethern which wer sent for in to the north

A lettere was sent to the Shiriffes of London for the honest
conveyng vp hither vpon hyred horses at the kinges costes
of Regnaldes of Shordiche and Jone Newman the wief
of Henry Newman of Harkeney

A lettere was sent to the Lord Chauncellour to desyre him to sende
hither with convenient spede a copye of Sir John Wythringtons
patentes for the office of Deputie Warden of the Marches
foranenpst Scotland

A lettere was sent to the Duke of Norffolk signifying vnto him
the receipt of his letteres to the Counsaill and also of the other
letteres to syr Cuchbert Ratclyff and John Ogle / And that
whereas the saide Duke practised the submission of the
Tyndales / by the forsayd letteres to syr Cuthbert and John Heron
the kinges pleasyr was that the counsaill here shuld breke
the matier / by mouth onely to John Heron / and that John
Heron at the cummyng in to the cuntrey shuld open the same
to Sir Cuthbert and soo they twoo Joyntly and severally
to perswade the Tyndalles to submit theimselfes to the kinges
highnes simpliciter without any parfaict assuraunce of pardon
addyng neuertheles that if they wold doo accourding to their
advise they shad such hope of the kinges mercye at the being

pagebreak


here of heron that they trusted by sum good sute to his highnes
vpon their humilitye might be moved to extende the same
towardes theim The Duke was alsoo desyred to aduertyse
the counsaill perfaitly whither syr Cuthbert Ratclyff
woold vndoubtedly take vpon him syr John Hoythringtons
office /


At Graston the xxixth of August
being present
The great Chambrelain of England
The greate Admirall of England
The Bishopp of Duresme
The Treasorer of the kinges household
The Master of the kinges horses
The Secretary Master Wriothesley
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Letteres wer sent from the Lord priuie Seale to the counsaill
the furst parte whereof required the dispeche of Thomas
Fouler of Calays for certain consideracions declared in
the saide letteres / The seconde parte made request vpon sute
of ceratin straungers inhabitauntes of Guldeford and
pharnam which having dwelt there long season wer
redy vpon thacte to departe to knowe the kinges determinate
pleasyr for their departure or abode / the thirde parte
declared the apparaunce of a certain prist of Windesour
called syr before him and others in his
cumpany at that tyme vpon the complaint of the Deane
of Wyndesour for certain wordes spoken by hym and
concluded his lettere with the sute of the keper of Wyndesour
Castell to be dischardged of pryson
there for speaking vnfitting wordes of the Quenes grace
which lettere and deposicion against the priste remayn
in the keping of Master Wriothesley Secretary /

Master Tuke wrote letteres bearing Date of
of the dispeche of Thomas Fouler and sent with the same
letteres the copye of the recognisaunce wherin the saide
Fouler and ix others / stand bounden in the hole which
copye and lettere remayn in my custody the clerk /

The said Thomas Foulwer deliuered to the Counsaill the
same tyme a certain conteynyng certain articles
touching aswell the payment of the kinges retinue
at Calays / and the Crew of Cvj men at Guysnes
as also the allowaunce of xxly markes by the yere to
be payed to the priste that singeth masse for the
warde and the allowaunce of letteres of Denyzens /
made by the commaundement of the Late lord deputie /
by vndre the hand oneley of the clerk of the counsaill there
which scedule is the aunswers totted vpon euery article
hed remayneth in the keping of Master Wriothesley
Secretary

Antony Auger paymaster to the kinges workmen of the
water workes at Dover / made sute for the somme of
sterling to paye with the same the wages there due
the And further declared
his opinion to be that considering the weakenes and age
of the Surveiour of the worke there / it shuld be expedient
for the kinges highnes to Joyne sum other with him that
might see his diuises and counpasses in the said workes
and if it shulde chaunce the said Surveiour to dye before
the perfection of the same that might go through and
make an ende of it

Sir William goringes Letteres written to Master Wriothesley
Secretarye for the obteyning of the kinges licence
to go home with his wief and alsoo other letteres from the
sayd Sir William Goring Jasper horsey and Wymonde
Carewe / Wherin sute was made to know the kinges
pleasyr touching the soiournyng of the Lady Anne of
Cleves the next Wynter / bicause of the making of
her provision for the household were red to the counsaill
and thervpon deliuered to the said Master Secretarye /

With the forsaide letteres the Lady Anne sent to the kinges
highnes a lettere sent to her in the Duytche tongue from
her brother the Duke of Cleve which she had broken
vp before and redd

The Vicar of Bamberie which at Notley vpon thaccusacion
in writting of Anthony Coope was commaunded vpon his
allegeaunce to be before the Counsaill this present Daye &
kept his apparaunce /

John Taillour of Rye put a supplicacion to the Counsaill declaring
in the same his wrong and long imprisonement at Diepe
for that in the kinges highnes syruice vndir Master Dudley vpon
the sees taking a certain frencheman in the podell at Rye
vpon suspicion of pyracie he caused him and his retynue
to be imprisoned / in the saide towne wheras a frencheman
and a frenche boye dyed Wherwithall the saide Taillour
is now chardged and kept in prison at Diepe and shalbe
hanged onles the kinges highnes provid of his goodnes
a remedy at the frenche kinges handes in that behaulf
For the frenche Ambassadour had written in his fauour
but his letteres toke none effect

The Lord great Master and the chief Justice of England
sent a lettere to the kinges highnes touching their procedinges
in the matiers of the forest with a scedule also therinclosed
declaring such articles / of waste of the kinges wooddes
as the tenantes about the forest had presented against
Sir William a parre/ amd hexam and had endited
him of the same /

being present
The great Chamberlain of England

John Heron was dispeched home and appoynted to haue
the rule bothe of Tyndale and Ryddesdale with the old
accustumed fees / and vndirtoke vpon payn of his lief
and g losse and gooddes / to see resitution made from tyme
to tyme / of all such gooddes and catalles as shuld be
taken awaye by any of theim of theim of Tyndale
or Ryddesdale / if the takers did not contynually abyde
in ScotLand /


pagebreak


An ouerture was also made and a licence gyven at the
same tyme to the saide John Lyeron as though it had bene
of the Counsailles owne hedd without the kinges knoweledge /
for a practise to be vse by him to perswade vj or viij
of those Tyndalemen which wer outLawes for the taking
and to offre to cum vp to the kinges highnes / submitting
themselfes vnto his grace / simpliciter / withou halters
about their neckes and the rest to submitt themselfes
there to the kinges Deputye warden / And was
commaunded vpon his arryvall there to conferre the
sayde practise / with syr Cuthbert Ratclyff and that
they bothe shuld signifie vnto the counsaill by their letteres
what conformitie they founde in the said Tyndalemen
to thintent they might thrvpon either sende their pardon
vpon the forsaid condicions orels they refusyng the saiid
maner of submission of theim to a conformitye / And
the said Heron had towardes his chardge by Waie of
the kinges reawrde / the somme of C;o

After the Dispeche of Heron Thomas Ogel and Cuthbert
Chaste being called in to the Counsaill and encouraged
to contynue in their good seruive / to the king had commaundement
to ayde and assiste John Heron from tyme to tyme when
he shuld call theim and wer so Dismissed and had
either of theim for their chardge vj li ciij s iiij d by Waye
of the kinges rewarde /

At the sute of Master Treasorer and Master Coferar of
househokd twoo placardes of one tenour were passed vndir
the Stampe for the authorising of Gurley
aswell to make provision of swannes partriches capons
chykens / etc for the kinges highnes vpon reasonable
pryces as also to take the same by engynes snares
nettes or otherwise / During the kinges abode at this
tyme at Grafton and Ampthill so that he shall
not medle / Within vj myles of the courte / nor in
places where restraint is made /

A lettere was sent to the Lord Chamberlain to Guisnes
signifying vnto him the kinges pleasyr was that he
shuld imedyatly Discharge his Crew of Cvj men which
he led ouer with him and that Thomas Fowler had
commaundement to see therin paied vntill the iijde day

The Mayour of the Staplers was commaunded by a lettere
and iij or iiij of the moost auncient of the Staplers
in the name of the rest to be at Ampthill the viijth
daye of Septembre next / and to bring with them such
pactes as passed Betwene the king and theim concerning
their shipping of wolles and felles to Calais /

The Deputie of Calais was aduertised from the counsaill
of their proceding here with Thomas Fouler and of
the Dischardge of the Lord Chambrelains Crew
of Cvj men /

The Lord priuie Seale was aunswered to the foure
poyntes of his Last lettere on the kinges behaulf as foloweth
Furst that a proclamacion shuld be sent furth out of
hande / Wherby Straungers borne / Being at this
present in the fee wages hyre ordynary dyes or retinue
of the king the Quene / the prynce the kynges other
children or the Lady Anne of Cleves be at liberty
to kepe such houses chambers and Seruantes as they
haue doon without any daungier of the Last acte or any
other actes And that thexecucion of the said Last act
against other straungiers now enhabitauntes thenne
next As touching the seconde point Fouleers articles
with the aunsweres totted vpon euery article wer sent
vnto him / Thirdly the king was contented that
he shuld enioyne the priste of Wyndesour to resyde
vpon his benifice / and to giue him a lesson to temperate
his tongue / hereafter vpon aduenture of further
punishement/ And finally concerning the prisoner

at Wyndesour that had spoken vnsuting woordes of the quenes
grace the kinges pleasyr was that he shuld yet remayn

At Grafton the Last daie of August the xxxij yere of the
Reign of our Soueraign Lord king Henry theight before
Robert Erle of Sussex Lord great Chambrelain of Englande
John Lord Russell Lord great Admirall of England
the Reverende father in god Cuthbert Bishopp of Duresme /
Sir thomas cheyney knight Lord warden of the cinque
portes and Treasorer of the kinges Secretarye Sir Richard
Riche knight Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions of the
Revenues of the crowne / the kinges Maiesties counsaillours
attendaunt vpon his personne / Richard Fermour
Late of London grocer / William Fermor of the towne
of Gomerton in the countye of Oxford Esquier / And
Thomas Lovet of Ashetwell in the parishe of Wapnam
in the countye of Northamptonshire Esquier / and euery
of theim by hymself recogniseth and knoweledgeth
themselfes and euery of theim to owe vnto our soueraign
Lord the king / the somme of one thousandes poundes sterling
to be levyed of their Landes tenntes gooddes and catalles

The Condicion of the saide recognisauncec as such that if
the abouebounden Richarde Fermour doo not at any tyme
or tymes / withoute the kinges speciall licence obteyned in
writting vndir his highnes great seale of Englande
Departe out of this his hignes Realme of Englande
in to any partes of beyond the see / And also if the said
Richard Fermour / do appere personally vpon a
reasonable warnyng / to be giuen vnto him at all
tyme and tymes before his highnes counsaill for
the tyme bein attendaunt vpon his highnes person
to aunswer to such thinges as shalbe Layed vnto him
that thenne this present recognisaunce shall stande
voyde and of none effect orels be in full strength
and vertue

Alsoo Jniunction was giuen by the Counsaill to the
saide Richard Fermour that he shuld not approche
in to the kinges presence at any tyme vntill he have
licence of his Maiestie to the contrary of and thervpon
in full counsaill dischardged from imprisonement
