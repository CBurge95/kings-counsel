At Moore the vth of Octobre
being present
The gret Chambrelain of England
The gret Admirall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Treasorer of household
The Master of the kinges horses
The Secretary master Wryothesley
The Secretarye Master Sadler
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Twoo Severall letteres wer sent by twoo yomen of the garde
to Rauff Roulet thelder of Saint Albons and to the baillifes
of the same towne / to send hither all the partrich takers takers/
with their gynnes and nettes swelling in saint Albons & theraboutes

Letteres wer sent to the Surveyours of the workes at Dover wherin
they alowed his submission and good conformite to satisfie the
kinges pleasir adcerteyning him of the kinges highnes fauour
and grace towardes him

The Chambrelain and Comptrollour of the princes household
wer sent for by letteres to be at the courte this next morowe /
being the vjth of this present at viij of the clock in the mornyng

Edward Bowland of thexchequier and Thomas Norton of London
grocer appered before the Counsaill and being examyned of the
bargeyn betweene Lee gentleman and the sayd Bowlande
of a leasse of Pollaxhill grainge belonging to the Late
Moner of Woborne / wer commaunded to attende bothe Boulande
and Norton daily from tyme to tyme vpon the Counsaill
vntill the matier wer better examnyed and the kinges pleasir
further knowen therin

Whereas William Poole of Calais in his sute to the kinges highnes
for the leasse of a house in Calais was referred to the counsaill
the same agreed in consideracion of pooles siruice to be meanes
to the kinges highnes by the mouth of the Master of the horses for
the obteynyng of pooles sute / And thervpon the said Master
of the horses tooke poole his bill of sute in to his handes /

Master William Peter Doctor of the Lawe was sworne to be one of the
kinges counsaill and appointed to that palce in the white haulle
that Doctor Thirleby elect bishop of westminster had before he was
