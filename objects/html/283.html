nouember the cownsell sate nott
for that they sate as commissioners
at the arreygnement off Culpeper
and derham


At westminster the first day off
december being than present
The lorde chawncellour
The Duke of Norffolk
The Duke of Suffolk
The lorde priuye seale
The lorde great chawmberlayne
Therle of Hertford
The bisshop of winchester
Sir Jhon gage Comptrollour
Sir richard riche chawncellour off thaugmentacions

All the vinteners apperid before the cownsell and wine for this yere was
than by them the said cownsell sett att vli the a toon of off the best and xiij no billes the worst

Where as v yonger brothers of the name of manwaringes had made theyre
complaynt to the cownsell of theyre eldist brother for convaying of his lande entaylid
as they pretendid to the vse of his dawghters, whereby they thowght theyr posse
bilite of succession in the saide lande defaytyd the knowlege of the hole matter
was remitted to the knowlege of the lorde chawncellour and the sayde eldist brother
was bounde in a recognisance of twoo hundreth pownde to appere the seconde
day of the next terme in the chawncerie

Strateley of the cowntie of oxforth gentleman hauing bin sent for by a
letter from the cownsell, for that itt was supposid that he knew muche of
the matter pretendid ageynst Jhon grevell of esquier of the same cowntye,
after he had made his answeres to certayne interrogatories deuised for
him on that behalff, gyving a corporall oth his confession to be trew
and that he knew no more of that matter was dismissed

A letter was sent to Sir thomas wriothesly than being at the cowrt
att Leddington, there attending on the kinges highnes

Sir Nicholas poyntz whiche before hadde bin committed to the flete and so
was restreyened from his b libertye that he might not passe the compasse of oon chawmbre
nor speake wyth any frende of his, was this day permitted to vse all kinde of
libertie within the sayde fleete, and s to speake with any his frendes that
thither wolde coom and visite him

Vpon certayne informations exhibited ageynst Sir thomas hanmer knight
a letter was directid to the president of the cownsell in the marches off
Wales for the triall of the trewth thereoff with commawndement that
in case thinformation sholde be fownde trew he shold apprehende him
and sense him vppe to the cownsell And in case the matter sholde
be fownde forged yett for disobeying a procept that the sayde
hanmer had to appere here the sayde president sholde take sureties
of him for his apperaunce the first day of the next terme

It was agreid that newdigate and
somtyme knightes of the religion
at malta sholde be dischargid off a recogni
sance wherein they wer bownde bec for theyre
apperaunce from day to day before the lorde
chawncellour and other the cownsell in the stirre
chawmber Entred by adam the clarke of the
sterr chawmber

Jhon grevell of the cowntie of oxforth esquier, having bin sent
for vpon a certayne heynous information ex exhibited ageynst
him, for that the tyme wolde nott serue, to trye the bottom of the matter
was for this present licensid to departe, boumde first in a recognisance
of ij li. tappere ageyne quindura hillary

The condition of this recognisance is thus, that if thaboueabownden
Jhon grevell appere before the cownsell attending vpon the kinges parson
at the seconde retourn of the next terme whiche shall be quindura hillary
and gyvw his attendance apon them from day to day tyll syche
tyme as he shall be by them dismissed than the sayde recognisance
to stonde voyde orells to stonde in full strength and vigewr

Twoo seuerall letters wer setforth the on for thapperance of
Jhon Bulwer and the other for thapperance of Alis Wilkes
otherwise callid rastholde

Newdigate and a nother somtyme of thordre of the rhodes where as
at theyre late comming in to inglonde they wer bownde by recongisance to app
appere from day to day wer dischargid of that bownde and licensid
to departe, bownde themselffes in a new recognisance to appere at all
tymes vpon a sufficient warning
