The deputie of Calays was aduertised fro by the
counsailes letteres of the recept aswel of his his letteres
to the kinges Maieste declaring aswel not only the admission of
Thomas Bridges in to the wages of viijd by the day
and also the vngentle proceding toward hym of Banestres one of the gentleman pencioners vngentle procedinges
towardes hym tooching for the bargayn of a certain horse
asalso h by false the recept of his other letteres toching the
to the counsail counsail written in his excuse & for that he sent not ouer an inventry of pates goodes sent lately brought from Calais and also for the declaracion
of his proceding with thomas Fouler for the victualling
and that the sayd letteres in which sayd letteres he from the counsail it was
signified vnto hym that as his procedinges concernyng bothe
thadmission of the bridges & his Discourse with fouler
for the victuallyng of guysnes wer moch to the
contentacion both of the kinges Maieste & of them all
so his Maieste did nether nother take in good parte
the doynges of we fouler tooching the sayd staple
& therfor willed the sayd depute to call vpon hym
for the satisfaction of herin dewtye in that behaulf
nother ye yet the intemperat language and mysbe
hauour which the sayd banestre had vsed towardes the hym the
sayd depute & therfor likers shuld be declared
more playnly vppon banestres retourne & therfor
it was thought declared vnto hym that for
the more pl evident convincing of the sayd banestre
it shuld be wel done that the sayd depute shuld
not only move the lord gray to aduertise the counsail
in wryting of the sayd banestres procedinges be also
to cause such four or fyve such honest persons
as save the prowd contenance & lewd demeanour
of the sayd banestre to signifie the same to the counsail
by ther letteres accordingly
