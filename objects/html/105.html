a lettere sent to the Sirveiour Comptroller & Paymaster of the
workes at Dover & to John Bartelet & John a Borowgh
gyving to them fyve the chardge of the workes & dischar
ging Maye & Justice Signifying further vnto them
that in case any variete of opinion shuld chaunce amonges
tehm for the practisyng of any devise that that opinion shuld
be put in execucion whervnto the most parte of them
shuld agre / and furthermore declaing to them that the
kinges highnes did allow vnto John bartelet & borowgh euery of
them xijd by the day duryng the tyme of there attendence there

Jone Benbury of the Countye of Dorset was examined of such
matier of treason as was Layed to her chardge by Dorethe
Lyte and denyng the sonne was remitted to the keping
of sir Thomas Arundel vntil such tyme as she could fynde
suertyes for her furthcummyng at the next sessions of gaole
delyvery there in the shyre to aunswer vnto such thinges as shuld be there
Layed there against her And the bills of the matyers Layed to
her charge was deliured to the said sir Thomas Arundell

Sir Oswald Wolstropp Leonard Edward Rawley and Robert Frybody
syruant to Edwarde Rawley wer examined for thescape of oon byrde
being noted to be syruant to the sayd Rawley & vehemently accused to be a felon for thapprehension
of which byrde Letteres wer written from Robert Master Southwell one of the nombre to try
the said Rawley And being suspect by the variety of their
aunswers to haue bene in confederacy with the said Byrde was
committed the said Rawley and his man to the tower and
Sir Oswald Wolstropp to the Marshalcy

A lettere was written to Master Richard Pollarde one of the generall surveiours to pay aswell vnto Fraunces
Haull for the boorde of the Lady Lisle and her three syruantes and
the wages of a prist after the rate of xxvjs viijd mere sterling
by the weke xlli and for the wages of thre syruantes during the said
tyme / and other necessaryes iijli xvs xd as alsoo to paye to foure
other persones for bourde of foure wemen for the space of xxxijten wekes
after the rate of vs the weke euery of them / amounting to xxxijli
which seuerall sommes make in the hole lxxvli xvs xd

Letteres wer written to Thomas Wingfeld Richard Dering and Thomas
Vaughan to vieu the workes at Dover and especially a certain barre
of prebill dryven in (as the Surveiour of the same workes gaue
enformacion by his letteres to the king) to the mouth of her borowgh
and to aduertise the kinges Maieste of the hole state of the workes
there accordingly

Seuerall letteres went sent aswell to therle of worcestre
the Lord Ferrers syr Richard Bulkeley knight william Vaughan
and Rowlande Morgan to to repair vp hither now at Candelmas
next to attende to attende vppon the Counsaill for thestablishment
of those partyes as alsoo to the president to send vp aginst
the saide tyme Master Pakington fully enstructed for those
purposes

The Condicion of this recognisaunce is such that if the
abouebounden William Pet doo personally appere before syr
Thomas Audeley knight Lord Audeley of Walden Chauncelour
of Englande and other the Lordes of the Kinges moost honourable
Counsaill in the Starre chambre at westminster the furst
Daye of Easter terme / next ensuyng and in the meane season
doo kepe the peax against all the kinges Subgietes and alsoo befor
the sayd Day not only to submitt himself to syr Thomas
Nevill knight asking him forgiuenes for offendyng him
but alsoo doo contente and pay for a cote which he did mangle
and cutt vnto the owner of the same cote such sommes or somme
of money as Master Willoughby the Judge shall before the
sayd Day award and iudge him to apt then etc. orels etc.

Thre seuerall letteres wer sent to sir Hugh Pollard
syr Hugh Paulet knightes and william Portman syrgeant
at the Lawe to repayre to Plymmouth there to wyne
with Master Trigonel in Commission for thexaminacion of an vnlawful
assemble / made there by thinhabitantes vppon a portugalles ship
and to doo further as the said Master Trigonell shuld aduertise
them in the kinges behaulf

Twoo seueral letteres wer written oon to syr John Fulforde
knight Shirif of Devon tattende vppon the saide Commissioners
and to doo as they shuld appointe him on the kinges behaulf
touching thadministracion of Justice for the said assemble / and
thother to Richard Edgecombe Richard Turbervyld Lewes
Fortescue / Esquyers / and John Thomas Mayer of Plymmouth
to giue enformacion to the said Commissioners and further
tende as they shuld assigne theim /

Twoo letteres of apparance wer sent the oon to Erneley
and thother to Rangingland

of Maydeston in Kent / put vp exhibited vnto the kynges Maieste a bill of complaint against doctour
Leffe Master of the colleage there and and oon walter herenden /
it was agreed that a lettere shuld be directed vnto syr Thomas
Nevill Sir Thomas Willoughby knightes Thomas Moyle
and Walter Henley Esquyers / to reapyre therhe furthwith vnto the

this next terme in the sterre chambre at westminster
