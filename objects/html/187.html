Wheras Antony Marler of London Marchaunt put vp a supplicacion vnto
the forsaid Counsaill in maner forme folowing /

Wheras it hath pleased you for the common wealth to take no
small peynes / in the furtherauncce of the price of my bookes Moost
humbly I before the same to haue in consideracion that onles
I have by the meane of proclamacion sum charge or commission
that church not redy prouided of one bible shall accourding
to the kinges highnes former Iniunctions / gyven in that behalf
priuide them of a bible of the largest volume by a day to be
prefixed and appointed as shalbe thought moost convenient
by your wisedomes / my grete sute that I haue made herin
is not only frustrate / and voyde but also being charged as I
am with an importune somme of the said bookes now lying on my
hande and vndone foreuer / And therfor trusting to the
merciful consideracions of your high wisedomes I humbly
desire tobteyn the same Commission of sum other commaundement
And I with all myn during our lifes ar and shalbe bounde
to pray contynually for your prosperous felicites long Endurace

Wherupon It was agreed by the lordes and others of the kinges Maiestes
Counsaill that their shalbe a proclamacion made according
to his request and that the day to be limited for the hauyng
of the saide bookes shall be hallowmasse /
