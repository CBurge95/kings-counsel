Therle of hertford beyng atd Calais and
the lord Matravers depute of the said towne wer
requyredby a lettere for the from the aforsaid Counsail to send ouer to
the tower of Lonond Harvey Comissary of
the forsaid towne and sir Thomas Tye prist
Which wer there deteyned in prison vppon matters
of Treason to thenten the sayd harvy & Tye
myght be further examined here vpon the same and
forasmocheas the parfait knoweledge of and prove
of the sayd Treasons layd to the charges of the
sayd harvy & Tye wer cam furst out by the
confession of the sayd Tye for the which at the sute
of the sayd lordes & others of the Counsail at
Calais it had pleased the kinges Maieste to promise hym
his pardon the sayd two lordes shuld therfore declare
to the sayd Tye secretly before his departure hitherward from
Calais the sayd goodnes of the kinges highnes to hym
& also gyve charge to such as had the conveyance
of them to vse hym honestly / keping the same from the
knowledge of harvy

Vppon complaynt made to the Counsail by the wardens of
the latten fownders in london that sundry persones vnder colour
of lycence to cary ouer the sees bel l metal did also cary ouer panne metal
laten metal & copper metal a lettere was affressed to the custumers
comptroller & sircheour at london aswel to sirche certain barelles
at the water side there tressed with metall & aduertyse the
wherof the sayd complaynantes shuld enforme them and thervpon to
aduertise the counsail what mettal wer tressed in the same asalso to take
ordre that no person do convey ouer by vertue of any licence any bel
metal before the same wer furst vieued & seen by an honest person

Stephen Fowle of the towne of Ospring in Kent
beyng sent for browght by a purswyuant was examined before
the counsail was examined tooching his misbehauiour vnto a post of
the kinges highnes which beyng sent in hast to Calais with his Maiestes
letteres and beyng destitute of a horse requyred the
sayd fowle having vj or vij horses in his stable
let hym haue one of them for his iorney to cantorbury
And the sayd fowle beyng convinced partely by his owne
confession & partely by the deposicion of others noted
to haue refused to letted lett the sayd post to haue
haue any of his horses let also to haue handled hym
self lewdly towardes the sayd post in taking from
hym his box wherin wherin wer the kinges Comission
& letteres but also in and callyng and reviling
the same with shameful & vnsemely Lernes was for
his punishement & to thexample of others committed
to the porters warde / and iudged
