Att saint James the xiiijth day off
April being than present as the day before

Certeyne ioyners whiche wer committed to warde for theyre vnlawfull
disguising vpon the sonday before wer this day after a good lesson vstred
to theyre libertie /

My lorde say off winchester my lorde saint Jhon and master wri Sir thomas
wriothesly being appoynted to hire and determyne a matter betwene Jhon
more gentylman and certeyne other parsons / fynding in thexamination off
thesame a great fawght to be in the sayde John More / for reformation
of thesame bownde him in recognisance as ensueth

The condicion of this recognisance is suche as yf theboue bownden John
More do not by any finisshe presse truble and vex the kinges highnes
Subiectes / nether commence nor entre any accion againste the same /
onles it shalbe in and for the a so cause grounded vpon iustice and
good matter to be followed and prosecuted in som of the kinges highnes
ordinarye courtes nether shall forge / contrive or consent to the forging
and contriving of any wrongfull informacion or sinistre presse to be
sued for vexacion of any the kinges highnes saide Subiectes wherby
any of the same maye be disquieted and putt to wrongfull bisniesse
nether shall medle with any matters, other then shall ether directlye
apperteyne vnto him Sealf or ells to the vpright administracion of
common Jvstcice / And that he cause the bridge newlie erected by him
over the comon River of Loden betwene the parisshes of Shirefelde
and hertely to be taken downe afore the First daie of maye
next commyng / Without any Bridge vpon the same water and
betwene the said parisshes by him to be new elected onles thesame
be doon by the agrement of thenhabitantes of bothe the saide
parisshes / that then etc. / or ells etc. /

A boke was exhibited to the cownsell by the mayour and recordour
etc. conteyning thexamination and depositions off all the butchers off
london towching the quantite off flesshe solde by them in lent / and to
what parsons they solde thesame

The condicion of this Recognisaunce is suche as where as it hath pleased
the kinges Maieste to license the saide John Fletcher to go to the Sees at his owne
adventure for reprisales to be taken of the frenchemen / yf the saide John
Fletcher do not in any wise take or spoile any of themperours subiettes
nor any other being in amytie with his Maiestie but only take his advantage
of frenchmen be way of Reprisall and of Scottes as ennemyes / And also
to make a certificat to the counseill vnder his hande writing before his
setting out what nomber of shipps and other vessells he well take with him
with the names and burden for the same and do withdrawe him Sealf
from the sees when he shall haue knowledge by proclamation or other wise
that the kinges Maiestie shall have adiuchiled this license and reuoked the saide
John Fletcher specially or generallye among others without attempting any
thing after the same shall com to his knowledg than etc. or ells etc. /

in very ill parte requiring him thereoff to dismisse thesame
incontinentlye wyth all gentle entreteynement

A lettere was written to the duke off Suffolcke to cawse
a proportion off wheat to be grownde in to flowre thesame to be
redye to be made in byskett by the first day off June / And caske
allso to be prouided to begynne to brew the xijth day off thesame /

Two women the one named Louell and the other
partriche wer examined off keping off open bordes off flesshe during
the Lent / and declaring themselffes nott to be so muche fawghtye
as was obiected ageynst them wer wyth a good exhortation dismissed

Where as Sir Jhon clere Thomas clere / wylliam stafford
and husey wer committed vnto the fleete for eating fleasshe
vpon good friday last past / there to be kept two and twoo in
a chawmber wythowt any other farther libertye This day itt was
ordred that they sholde haue the libertye off the garden /

My lorde Cobham and my lorde Clynton wer called before
the cownsell / and being there examined off eating off flesshe
and and keping an open borde thereoff during the lent
alleging first for the eating thereoff a license of opteyned
off the king and denyyng the abuse thereoff in suche sorte
as was layed vnto theyre wer wyth a good Leasson dismissed

Sir Edward baynton Rogers Jhon sowche warner
and gawen Carrow wer in like sorte examined off the matter
abouerehersed / and parte off them alleging a license opteyned
for the same / and parte off them knowlegyng theyre fawghtes whiche
they toke to haue ben off suche wayght / humblye submitting
themself to the kinges highnes plesur / wer after a good exhortation
geven vnto them dismissed /
