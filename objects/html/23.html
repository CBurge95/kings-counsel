At Ampthill the viijth of September
beyng present
The grete chamberlayn of England
The grete admyrall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Treasourer of household
The Master of the horses
The Secretarye Master Wryothesley
The Chauncelou of thaugmentacions

Sir Rafe waren knyght Mayer of the Staple and
with hym Mychael Dormer William Dantzey Williams
Robyns and John William Butler marchantes of the Staple appered
before the Counsail and delyuered to them to be pervsed
two Indentures one with the kinges grete Seale of England
appendant and an other without any maner Seale at it
And wer comaunded to be eftsones before the Counsail
at the xth of this present monethe /


At Ampthill the ixth of September
beyng present
The grete chamberlayn of England
The grete admyrall of England
The byshop of Duresme
The Treasourer of household
The Maister of the horses
The Secretary master Wryothesley
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions etc.

Letteres wer brought From the Duke of Norfolk to the Counsail
declaring the recept of letteres vnto hym From Master Pate
concernyng the como cummyng ouer of the sayd philip ap
henry otherwise called philip apary othwerwise called
vaughan which philip cam also at this tyme to the
courte out of the partes of beyonde the see wheras he had been
of long season aswel in the cumpany of poole asalso of
James ap howell whose dowghter he had maryed at
Reginsbowrgh / and beyng ones examined he was sett

pagebreak


at libertie and had commaundiment to attend dayly vpon
the Counsail /

Letteres was also brought from the lord privey seale declaring
a certayn affray to be made by syr Geoffrey poole
in hampshyre vpon one master Gunter a iustice of peax
for that (as poole sayd) one of Gunters sirvantes had
spoken evill of hym and for that also that hymself
Gunter had disclosed to the kinges Counsail in the tyme
of pooles trouble certain secret conderence which poole
had with hym / and answer was made to the sayd lorde privey
seale that calling the complaynt eftesones before hym
the lordes & others the gentlemen & Justices of peax in the
cuntrey to thentent the crymes of syr Geffrey may myght
notorious to all the cuntrey there he shall shuld commytt the
said sir Geffrey to the flette to remayne there vntil
further knowelege of the Kinges pleasir


At Ampthil the xth of September
beyng present
The grete chamberlayn of England
The grete admyrall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Treasourer of household
The Master of the horses
Sir Thomas Wryothesley Secretarye
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Aduertisement was sent to the duke of Norfolk of the
recept aswell of his letteres toucheing sir Cuthbert Ratcliff to
be depute warden of the west Marches and as and
of others toochyng the commendacion of a prist in Norfolk
a surgeon to be made denyzen asalso of those which
concerned philip ap henry / The furst was answered
that the patent was redy to be sent at Mychaelmas
to sir the sayd sir Cuthbert The seconde lettere it was
answered that a convenient a tyme shuld be taken betwene this
and Easter wher for my the sayd Duke & the rest of the
Counsail to sue to the King for the sayd prist /


pagebreak


Tooching the thirde Lettere he was aduertised that
philip ap henry had bene by the kinges commaundement
before htem nad that perceyving as yet litle frute in
his tale they sestt hym at libertie charging hym to be
ones a daye before them to thentent that as occasion
shuld sirve they myght sucke sum materill thing
of hym /

A lettere was sent to my lord Chauncelour that the
Kinges pleasir was that Master bownden shuld in any
wise enioye the benifice called gillingham in kent which his highnes had of late
gyven hym notwithstanding any title that my the lord
Chauncelour made to the gyving of such benefices as by
virtue of his office that were vndre a certayn somme
with advise from the Counsail to stay in such maters / and
vntil he had spoken with the kinges highnes in it and that
also to forbeare makyng of denizens vntil he had spoken
with the kinges highnes in those maters /

A lettere was sent to the chief Justice of the commen
place answering his written before tooching an affray
betwe betwene duaceyt and lete wherof faucet did
for the Counsailles advise tooching the bailyng of faucet
beyng in pryson for the slawghter of one lyte

At Ampthil the xiijth daye of
September beyng present
The grete chamberlayn of England
The grete admyrall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Treasourer of householde
The Master of the horses
Sir Thomas Wryothesley Secretarye
The Chauncelour of thaugmentacions

It was declared to John apryce that the kinges highnes
had determyned he shuld have the office of Secretary to with his
his highnes with m COunsail in the marches of wales with the
fees of xxte markes by the yere and thallowance of such other
diettes as other Secretaryes there before hath had / And
further it was declared to Charles Fox that the kinges pleasir
was that he shuld enioye the offices of Clerke of
the Signes & Clerk of the Counsail according to theffect

pagebreak


of his highnes patent fraunted to hym & hys brother edmund
Fox in that behaulf / And ordre was taken that all such
maters of variance as wer depending betwene so them sayd
pryce & Fox for the sayd offices shuld be nowe ended & eyther
discharge thother of all sutes in that behaulf /
