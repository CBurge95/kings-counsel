Wheras tharchebishop of Cantorbury had aduertised the
Counsail that a lewde of Cantorburye had deuised a false
bill and set to the same a false seale purporting a graunte
vnto him of one of the beademen in Cantorburye / ordre
was taken and signified by a lettere / to the saide Archebishop
that he shuld cause one of the saide felowes eares to be
nayled to the pillorye the next market daye at
Cantorburye / and there to stande during the market
with a paper / conteyning in gret letteres the cause of his
punishement /

A lettere was addressed to the custumers Comptroller and
Serchour of Yamouth to permitt and suffre James Sutton
one of the clerkes of the grenecloth who is appointed to make
prouision of victaill for the Souldyours and workmen at
Calais and Guisnes / to sende over from tyme to tyme
all such victail as he shuld provide for the saide purposes
without paying custume for the same or fynding suertyes
for the delyvery therof at Calais /

The Deputy of Irlande was aduertised by a Lettere
addressed vnto him / to Barry Welshe Johon Mynne and
william Cauindishe Commissioners sent fromthens that the
kinges Maieste was contented that the th saide thre commissioners
shuld retorne in to Englande after the dispech of their dispeche
of their busines there so as at there being here they shuld make
with diligence a transcriupte of their bookes of Survey to be sent
again in to Irlande / by the saide Cauindishe whom the kinges
pleasyr is shall returne in to Irlande to be there at such
tyme as the vicetreasorers accompt shalbe made /

The xviijth day of June the
Counsail did not sit
