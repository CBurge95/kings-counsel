A Lettere was written to Robert Southwel Esquier
willing him to sende to the towre certain bedding and such other
thinges for the necessary relief of Mason as was expressed in
a scedule conteyned in his said lettere the double of which lettere
remayneth in the keping of syr Thomas Wriothesly Secretary

A lettere was written to John Piers clerk of the Check of
the garde / to deliure to Robert Southwel the Maler and
bought with all thinges conteyned in the same which he toke in
to his custody at thapprenhension of Mason

John Augustyn and Richard Hilyar wer examined before the
Counsail of the bill of complaint put in against Sir Thomas
Dennys knight the quenes Chauncelour and Augustyn being
sworne to aunswer to certain interrogatories touching the said
bill was remitted to me the Clerk of the Counsail to take his
deposition vppon the same and hylyare appering to haue no Richard Forasmuch as it was thought by
knoweledge in the sayd matyer the Counsail that he had no knoweledge in the said matier was dimissed
