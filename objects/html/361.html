August being than present
Tharchebisshop off canterbury

Sir rafe Sadlair secretary

A lettere was sent to sir Jhon baldewyn chief iustice
of this commen place certifying the receyte off his letteres and
certeyne depositions wyth the same / and sygnifiying the the
matter semed nott to them of suche weyght as he sholde nede
to troble himselff therewyth any farder

A lettere was sent to the duke off norffolk for the spedie prouision
and sure convaying to barwyke of v quarter of wheate
v quarter off rice and a thowsande quarter off barley
Signifiyng farther that in consideration off his besnesses
he might for atyme be absent from the court

The same day vpon certeyne aduertisementes from the nroth
a lettere was sent to the duke off norffolk, to repayre the next day
to the court

A lettere was sent to the lord chawncellour to direct commissions
to all sherefes and iustices off peace thorowghowt the realme
to make musters incontinentlye / and certifye the nomber

A lettere was sent to sir Antony kingston to desist and
ceasse from ministering and taking any man wythin the
forrest off deane

The same day a lettere was directed to gennyns viceadmirall
of the shipps that now be on the see declaring vnto him
the kinges highnes pleser for the ordering of suche men of
war as he sholde probably perceyue to be robbers on the see
or to haue ben busye wythin the kinges stremes or otherwyse
so to houure and lye before the costes hannes or crakes off
off englonde as the trade off marchandise myght and entre in
to the sayde haures might therbye be empeched ot interrupted /


Att hamptoncourt the xxvth day

A lettere was sent to the mayour off norwyche wyth commawnde
ment to deliuer to diego Estudillo suche gooddes off his as remayned
in the sayde towne / lately in the occupation off
george Aylenale his factour / than deceased /

A lettere was sent to the mayour off bristow at the suit
of sir thomas Arondell / for to se iustice ministered on
the behalff of a seruant of the sayde Sir thomas keper
of Sande parcke /

A lettere was deuised and sent to my lorde chawncellour
to make proclamations thorowghall the see costes / than noman
by vertue off any better for the levying off men sholde take
vppe for that intent any mariner /

Letteres wer sent to richard Lee surveyour off Callais
to vew diligently suche trenches as lately had ben made by the
frenchemen night vnto arde / and to certifye what hurt thesame
might do ether to the new ryver or ells to any other parte off the pale
