Jheronimo strawngier appered according the cownselles
commawndement / and bringing in his answer in writing
for that itt appered that the matters partely wer vntrew
and partely off no great importance / and that the gray
fryers being now apperteyning to the kinges highnes
the officers off the towne ought nott to attempt the serche
off any howse situate wythin thesame / After a good
lesson gyven him to obserue all common orders and so
to order himselff as he might deserue the loue and

Letters wer sent to the lorde deputye off callais and
the rest off the cownsell towching certeyne laborars fledde from the
worckes to flawnders / Item towching snowden / and the demisens
there to remayne in theyre owlde estate till the kinges
pleasir sholde be farther knowen /

A Letter was sent to sir Cuthbert radclif certifyng him
that the kinges highnes had taken his late aduertisementes
in good and thankfull parte / and etc. /
