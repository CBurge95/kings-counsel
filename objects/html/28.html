At Ampthil the xvjth of September
beyng present
The lord gret Chamberlayn of England
The byshop of Duresme
The Treasourer of household
The Master of the horses
Sir Thomas Wryothesley Secretary
The Chauncelour of thaugmentacion

A Lettere sent to Master Pate in to flawnders answering to those
sent from hym before to the kinges Maieste tooching the pardon of
Henry Philip ap henry sent from Master Pate who had bene
long beyond the sees in the cumpany of James Griffith ap
howell & Reginald poole /

A Lettere sent to sir John alen knyght tooching the counsailles
opinion for the bailling of sir John Clerk parish prist of
asheford in the countye of Middlesex browght before hym for
speking certayn vnsemyly woordes as apperith by the deposicions
of certain persones contenyed & in wrytynges and remaynyng
in my custody

Richard Rawnshawe sirgeant at armes appered
before the Counsail ans answered that he had not done
satisfied their commmaundement prescribed vnto hym in
their letteres concernyng the delyuery to master Chauncelour of thaugmentacions of such evidences as he
or any other man to his vse had in their kepeing concernyng
the Landes of Katherin Tattersal wydow beyng lunatik
and the also the delyuerye of the rentes which he had receyved of the
sayd landes to alisander boloygne For that he had never
rece or any other man to his vse by his meane or procurement
never had receyved one peny profet of the say landes
not hath in t any peace of wrytinges or evidences concerning
the same /

It is was ordered & enioyned that by the Counsail that
the forsayd Richard Rawnshawe Sirgeant at ares beyng then present before ye counsail that he shall in no wise
medle of have to do with the goodes catalles landes tenementes
of or bodyes of Katheryn Tattersall wydowe her
soone or dowghters but delyuer before thre of the clock
after none to morowe beyng the xvijth of September the sayd
aswell the bodye of the sayd Katheryn & as also the possession
of all her goodes & landes / And that further it

was ordered that Letteres shuld be written to the master of the

John Heron the bastard brother of Gyles Heron was
by king the porter eftesones browght before the
Counsail and after his examinacion the same beyng
the right knowledginges his foly in vsing of faf
fantastical practises in astronymye was set at libertie and
was bownd in a recognisaunce of one hundred marke in forme
folowyng /

The condicion is that if the sayd John Heron do not at any tyme from
hensforth practise vse or exercise any maner of neg
necromancye astronymye calculacions or other experimentes
and also do from tyme to tyme discover and open
to sum one of the kinges Counsail all such as he
shall at any tyme herafter know to vse or practise the same
and also do apeare vpon a convenient warnyng
in such maters as hath alredy at this tyme bene obiected vnto
him that then etc.

Letteres were sent to my lord Chauncelour aduertising
sending vnto hym of the two de letteres patentes of denyzens
which wer sollen owte and also of the charge that Arnold
Albert and his felowe had to appere before hym to purge them
selfes in that mater /

Letteres were sent to Thomas Fouler of Calais for the
payment vnto Guysnes of his wages vntil the kinges pleasir
to the contrary wer knowen

Letteres sent to the president & counsail in the marches of wales
declaring the kinges pleasir tooching the admission of John
ap rice to be secretary there and Edmund Fox & Charles
Fox to be Clerkes of the Signet furthwith and clerkes
of the counsail when the same shuld fall voyde /
