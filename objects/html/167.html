John Hillary of Kaynsham / who did exhibite a supplicacion
to the kinges Maieste declaring in the same that Master
Chauncelour of theaugmentacion having enfourmacion of him
that the kinges highnes was deceyved by the Late Abbot
of kaynsham of CC markes yerly did not in the tryall
therof as apperteyned to his duety was herd before the
Counsaill in the presence of the said Chauncelour / what
he could saye touching that mattier / And appering
manifestly to have slaundersouly and vntruely forged the
said mattier / against the said Chauncelour / was committed
to the Marshalsy for his punishement

And wheras John Gunter william wayte and John Enely
of Chichestre Esquyres aduertised the Counsaill by ther
letteres / that accourding to the purport of a lettere directed vnto
them from the said Counsaill / that they had caused
the whete Laden in a certain flemishe hoye stayed their
by them to be brought on londe and sold the same
at vjs viijd the quarter / to be payed at the feast
of penthecost next ensuynge / A lettere was sent vnto
the said Gunter & Wayte wherby and Enely / requyring them
that forasmuch as there was appered a playn bargain betwene
the owners of the said hoye and one Bowyer of
Peteworth / for the sayd hoye and fourty quarters
of wheate And that for thaccomplishment therof
they brought the ship in to the haven there / they were requred to delyver
vnto the said Fleminges the said hoy and all other
thinges conteyned in the same / and allso to take such
ordir with them as the money for xl ty wuarters of wheate
after the rate a they had sold ithe same / might be
satisfied at their appointement at the Dayes agreed
for the payment therof / and wheras there was laden
in the said hoye xviij quarters of wheat more then
was entred in the cocket of the Custumers of Southhampton
forasmuche as the same wes plainly forfaited by the
kinges maiestes Lawes / they wer requyred to receyve the moy
to be made of the said wheate therof and deducting the chardges for
binging it on Lande after the rate of the quantity
to convert the one half to his Maiestes vse & the other haulf
to such a wer the Seysons therof of the same

Wheras syr Geoffray poole knyght had violently
and contrary to the kinges highnes peax assauted & hurt
syr John Mychaell Clerk passon of Racton in the countye
of sussex as appered by a supplicacion exhibited vnto the Justices of thise And thervpon an iniunction directed vnto
hym vndre the gret Seale of for the consyruacion
of the peax Master Shelley one of the Justices of the commynserch was
requred not only to call before hym & other the kinges
Justices at the next sessions the sayd syr Geoffray & to commande
not only to put in sufficient suretyes of for thobsiruacion of
the sayd Iniunction but also to answer vnto the sayd bill
of supplicacion / And the to certifie vnto the counsail
theyr procedinges in the same

Thomas Marriot of Rugby in the county of Warwik
who was accused to haue spoken certain Lewde and
vnsemely woordes of the kinges Maieste / being examined
before the said Counsaill of the asme and appering
to haue spoken the said woordes rather of a madnes
and frenesy then of any rancour or malice was dimissed
with a lettere to Master Wigston and certain others Justices
of the peax there to cause such gooddes of his as wer
attached vpon his arrest to be restored vnto
him accordingly /
