Where as the mayour and aldermen of oxforth had written to the cownsell
towching certayne seditiows wordes spoken by on Jhon Willison taylour saying
he sholde se or he died friars and monkes vppe ageyne etc and desired to
know the plesir of the sayde cowncell towching the ponisshement of the sayde
willison They determined that on the next markett day after the date
off a Letter whyche was written to the sayde Mayour and his brother towching
the same, they sholde sett him vpon the pillory, there to stonde from the be
gynnyng of the markett tyll the ende wyth a paper vpoin his vpperrest
garmentes declaring his offense and that don after on dais emprisonement
and a good lesson tolde him by the hed officers to he sholde to be sett at his libertye

A lettere was directid to the deputye and the cownsell off irlande to
make som reasonable ende towching a bill of complaynt sent to them
withall
