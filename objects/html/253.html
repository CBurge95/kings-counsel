A certain complaint exhibited by Thomas Scaueceby
a grome of the kinges chamber against tharchebishop of Yorke
wherin was conteyned that not onely and the said Archebishop and others had intruded
and forcibly entred vpon the Kinges hospitall as the said Scaveceby pretended
of Kynwoldegraves in the County of York but also that
the said matier being brought before the barons of thexchequr
the same had not proceded therin for the Kinges Maieste indifferent
which mater being maturely examyned and aswell the said
Scaveceby as the sayd Archebishop herd togidres herd
at length what he could alledge for the kinges title
Forasmuch as it appered he could neither shew any
evidence in watting prescription of tyme nor any other other
indifferent prove that the Kinges highnes had title
to the sayd house and that therfor it did appere that
he had not onely imagyned this matier craftly & vntruely
to vexe iniustly the said Archebishop but also that in his
procedinges in the sayd iniust vexacion he did aswel entreprise
certain vnlawfull attemptates as also behave & vse himself

towardes the president and kinges counsail here and to

and a wrongfull troubler of the kinges Subgietes
the sayd lord of the priuey Counsail by speciall commandement
of the kinges highnes have have & do decreed that the said Scaveceby
shuld be committed to warde there to remayn during the kinges

discharged of the kinges syrvice

Tharchebishop of York was commaunded to cause
all the shrynes with their hovels and all other their

and the places wherer they stode to be made even
