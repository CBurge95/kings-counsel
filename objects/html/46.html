At Moore the ixth of October
being present
The Lord pryvey Seale
The Erle of hertford
The gret Admirall of England
The Treasorer of household
The Master of the horses
Sir Rauff Sadler knight Secretarye

George Whelpeley put in to the Counsaill a certain booke
of complain against the custumers and Comptrollers and
Serchours of the sundry portes for their vntrue dealing in their
offices towardes the kinges Maieste

Sir John Gage knight was admitted Comptroller of the kinges
household in the placee of Sir william kingston knight Lateley decessed

Letteres wer written to the Lord Chauncelour aswell for the
dispeche of John ap Rices patent of the office of Secretary
in Wales as alsoo for the sendyng hither of an abbreuiate
of all the offices and fees with the Names of them that
haue them at this present in the Chauncery kinges
Benche or commyn place / And furthermore he was
requyred to send an iniunction at the sute of william
Willoughby of Orford in Suffolk vnto the tenantes of
the sayde Orforde / that they shuld suffre the said
Willoughby to enioye his inheritaunce in Orford &
Sudborne / vnto such tyme as the variaunce betwene
them for the same wer determined before the counsaill in the sterre chambre /

A lettere was sent to the Deputye Treasorer and Comptroller
of Calais to deliure the possession of the house &
gooddes of the Lady Banestre decessed which they
had ceased to the kinges vse / vnto Banestre
her sonne in Law for the vse of himself and others
that had right to the same / byndyng him in a
sufficient somme of money for the fourth cummyng
of the sayd house and gooddes in case that any
thing within a twelfmoneth canne be alledged
for the king wherby his highnes shuld haue
interest in the sayd house and gooddes

A lettere was sent to Master Pate declaring vnto
him that notwithstanding the cancred & traitorous
lettere that hylyares sent vnto him & his chaplain
wherby sum suspicion might haue bene concerned
the kinges highnes did conceyve right good opinion
of the trouth and fidelity both of himself & of his
chapelain towardes his Maieste wherin he might
a without being dismayed being repose himself
assuredly

A lettere was sent to diuerse of the lordes of the
pryvey counsaill being at London in commission for
the subsydy aduertising them of the kinges determinacion
for the sending for of the Lady Kempe & Masteres
vnto them to London and no further / and vpon
conformyty to satisfie the kinges pleasir to sendyng
them back imedyately to their owne houses for the

pagebreak


setting of an order and staye in the same/ tappointe
to repayre assone as they might conveniently thother to
the court wheras they shuld be furt being further
aduertised of the kinges pleasir in that behaulf
ye shuld furthwith departe fromthens to the plae
appointed for their abode

Four severall letteres wer written to the Master of the
wardes The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions and
tenthes and to the generall Surveyours for the sendyng
hither of an abbreuyate of all the offices and fees
with the Names of them that haue then at this
present within the Limittes of their severall offices


At Moore the xijth of Octobre
being present
The Lord priuey Seale
Therle of hertford
The Comptroller of household
The Master of the horses
Sir Rauff Sadler knight Secretary

Pryvey Seales wer directed by the ordir of the said Counsaill
vnto Robery Gaywood Customer Lord Serchour of Oypsroyche to William
Gaywood of Malden and Thomas Gaywood of Sudmestre his brethern vndersirrchours
in Essex to appere vpon their allegiaunce before the Counsail
vpon the receipte of the same /

Letteres wer sent to Harr William Harrys of Essex to put ordre
for the same keping of certain butter & chese of the gooddes
of william van Tongres straunger seised by Elys brooke / to
the kinges vse / if the same could be proved forfayte and
otherwise to the vse of the partye

Doctor Peter one of the kinges ordinary Counsaill was called for before the Counsaill to here what
John Barkley of Cantorbury could saye vnto him face to face
touching the conceilement of a certain book of treasons f put
vnto him against the prior of Christeschurch in Cantorbury at the
tyme of his visitacion iij yeres ago And forasmuch as the said
Doctor Peter denyeng thaccusacion to be true / the sayd barkley

pagebreak


could not prove the same / saing that having herd the same
matyer spoken at Cambridge by the montly of sir James piers
Late monke of the sayd church he did put in the sayd
enformacion onely for his owne dischardge / he was commanded
to appere eftsones before the sayd Counsaill Crastino animo
and to bring in vp all such proves as he could aswell touching
the sayd article aginst Master Peter as alsoo other articles
put hin by him against John Antony of Kent /

Letteres wer sent to the Duke of Norffolk aduertising him
of whelpelers accusacion against the Custumers with reque
as of letteres sent from Master Pate to the king & his
Counsaill and others sent vnto himself which wer sent
vnto him at this tyme / and further he was desyred
to cause to be sent hither an Abbreuiate of all the
offices & fees being vnder thoffice of Treasorer of England
with the Names of them that haue & occupye the same
at this present
