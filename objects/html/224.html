Henry Forescot and william Hudson being eftsones
brought before the Counsaill and hauing a good lesson
gyven then to vse themselfes herafter more discretely
wer discharged and set at libertye /

A lettere was written to the Mayer of Newcastle &
Thomas Cary to the custumers comptroller & sircheour there that they shuld restore again vnto John
Davyson marchaunt of Scottland not not only aswell his ship and all her called
the John of Careil taklyng and all her sailes takling which they had restrayned there as forfait
but also al his apparaill with her sailes & taklyng asalso the a
appareill and be and bedding apperteynyng asw vnto the said
Davison or the the master of the shipp or the maryners with all such
other thinges as wer in the said shippe the money only
excepted which said shippe was restreyned by the
sircheour there as forfait for that there was certain
money fownd in her redy to haue bene conveyed contrary
to the statutes provided in that behaulf /
