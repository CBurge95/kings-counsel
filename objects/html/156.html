At Westminster the xviijth of Daye of
Marche being present
The bisshop of Cantorburye
The Lord Chauncelour of England
The Duke of Norffolk
The Lord pryvey Seale
The gret Chambrelain of England
Therle of herford
The bisshop of Duresme
The vicechambrelain
Sir Thomas Wriothesley knight Secretary

The condicion of this recognisance is such that if the
John Hoorde of London sonne and heyre of Richard Hoorde
of the county of Salop Esquyre do personally appere vpon a lawfull warning
at all tymes within a twelf moneth now next
ensuyng / before the Lordes and others of the kinges
highnes counsail attendaunt vpon his personne and
also do obsirue fulfill and kepe all such orders
decrees and determinacions as he the sayd John
shalbe denied adiudged or commaunded to do by the
sayde Counsail that then etc / orels etc /

The condicion of this recognisaunce is such that
if the sayde Morgan Welles and John Blackell
do personally appere before the kinges Maiestes counsail
attendaunt vppon his personne vpon a lawful warning at all tymes betwene
this and the xvth day after Easter next ensuyng
that then etc orels etc /

Wheras Thomas Dawes of Ildersley in the countye of derby
laborer had accused sir Robert moore prist parson of brodley in the said county and
a certain woman beyng in howse with the sayd prist of sundry haynous
and traitorous spo wordes supposed by the sayd Thomas
to be haue bene spoken by the sayd prist & woman as
and thervppon certain letteres wer written from hamptoncourte the iijde of the present to therle of
shrewsburye to examyn the sayd matter to apprehend the
said parties & to examyn the matter forasmocheas as doth
appere before by letteres written from the counsail
foasmoche it apperith by the answer of the sayd Erle and
thexaminacions taken by hym that the prist & woman wer
in nowise culpable of such thinges as wer laid to
theyr charge the said Erle was requyred by
an other lettere to discha not only to discharge
the sayd prist & woman incase there could be no
further profe or euydence browght against then
for the proves of such matter as was obiected
again vnto them but also to cause the sayd
Thomas Dawes openly in the parish church of the
said prist to aske hym forgyvenes vppon his
knees knoweledging that he had faulsely and
maliciously slawndred them and if he refused
so to do then to cause hym to be sett vppon the
pillory the next market day in example
of such malicious faules knows as myght herofte
attempt to do the semblable

John Hurley siruant to Culpeper one of the signet of the
kinges highnes privey chamber John Cousyns and John Hubbard
siruantes to Yomen siruantes to paston
another of the gentlemen of his Maiestes privey chamber
be was browght before hte cousnail for makyng
of a riottous & vnlawfull assemble and affraye
in sowthwerk & beyng bothe by theyr owne confession
& also others sufficient witnes convynced to be fawty
& culpable of the same wer committed to the flytte

A lettere was written to the lord warden desyring
hym that wheras thinhabitantes of Rye had taken
and imprisoned a certain man of Deapre in such sorte
as thowght they had had a lettere playn reprisall &
lettere of Mark (likes as be a lettere sent from the
frenche ambassadour to the lordes of the consail
apperith which lettere was also sent to the sayd lord
warden) he woold take such spedy redresse therin
as to Justice shuld apperteyne

Thomas Barnabe a siruant one of the kinges which
highnes siruantes which had commission certain thing &
in doyng for his Maieste in the parties of beyond
beyng retorned hither for the declaracion of his
procedinges in the same and doubting to be vexed
of sundry persons aswel of london as eles where
vppon private sutes had a supersedeas vnder the consailles
had to the Maiour of london etc Sherif aldermen & shyriffes
of london & to all other the kinges highnes offices not
only to permitt & hym to passe & repasse to & from the
counsail at al tymes vntil he wer discharged dimissed
from the same but also without any maner sute or troble
but also in case they had arrested or attached any
of his gooddes they shuld cause the same to be redelyuered
vnto hym accordingly

A commandement was dyrected from the Consail
to all maner the kinges the Maiour of Northampton
& to all other the kinges officers to ayde and
assiste Rafe Holfer & John Belson two of the
yemen of the garde for the prosenteng from place
to place & to take one gregory Myllet a
felon & to thentent they he myght bring the same be browght
before the sayd Counsail by the sayd two yemen of the
gard with all suche money wrying Jeweles & writinges
as shuld be fownd about hym at the tyme of
his apprehension /
