At Grenewiche the xiiijth of april
beyng present the forsaid Counsail except
tharchebishop

Wheras certain evill disposed persones vnknowen had
burned in the countye of Surrey a frame of tymber belong
ing to syr John gressam of london knyght a lettere was
addressed to Justie willowghby & certain other Justices
gentlemen dwelling in the sayd countye to enquyre at
the sessions at godstone vppon the tewsday in ester weke
now next ensuyng and to examyn all such persones as they
shuld think suspecious to thentent to knoew of the sayd
fact & in case they had knoweledge of the same to committ
them to warde / & to certifye the counsail of theyr proceding
in that behaulf

Wheras John Smyth one of the kinges highnes
watermen & certain others watermen had lately dutied
a ship of portugalle lyng before fauersham in Kent called the antony of Cindy
in luxbone and vndre pretense of a seisure of
marchandize in the same forfayted (as they sayd for
lack of Custumer) and did breke vp the master and
Maryners sh Cofers & so tooke awaye diuerse parcelles
of sundry kyndes of thinges forasmocheas vppon the
re and also the same Smythe & others aforsayd at
that sayd same tyme tooke with them from the sayd Rode
two cy vnto london two carrches laden with corne
of one John antonyes / Forasmoche vppon the
examinacion of the sayd two severall seisures it
appered that the gooddes taken by them out of
the portugalles shippe was only thinges belonging
to the Maryners bowght by them for theyr owne
wearing & vse and that it is knowen that mary
ners in all places cary such thinges with them without
payment of custume / It was ordered that the sayd
Smyth shuld delyuer vnto the sayd portugalles incontynetly
all such thinges as he taken from the beyng specified
in a scedule annexed to thexaminacion of the same
matter And that as tooching the wh catches of
whete they sayd Smyth shuld there take the such
avantage as the order of the kinges Lawe woold
awarde /
