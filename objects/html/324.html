Vpon an humble petition made and exhibityd by Johanne
bulmer wife to bulmer esquier / that where the sayde
Bulmer enioyed by the saude Jhone bulmer his wife
oon hundreth marcke by yere / and therev and vpon now occasion
refusing her companye thereoff allowed her nothing / An order
was taken by the cows cownsell that forasmoche as the sayde
Bulmer alleged himselff to be farre endetted he
sholde the monday next ensuing the date hereoff bryng and
present to the cownsell the particulars off the same / And neverthelesse
itt was ordeyned that bulme he sholde permitt the sayde Jhon
bulmer his wyfe to soiourne for oon yere att her brothes
hows namid Arworth off lewton / Allowing her for
the sayde yere xxti marckes for hir borde / and xxti other marckes
for hir nexessarie expenses / And in cace he sholde during
the sayde terme haunt and resorte to her / and vse her after
suche a convenient sorte as itt bihoueth one an honeste man
to vse his wife / than apon relation made off the same at the
yeres ende an order sholde be taken aswell for the relesse off
the payment off this sayde money to her as to haue her
and to appoyne her for her demourre where he sholde thinkes
