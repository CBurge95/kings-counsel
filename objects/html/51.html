At More the xvth of October
beyng present
the lord privey Seale
The Comptroller of household
The Master of the horses
Sir Rafe Sadler Secretary

Letteres wer browght to the Counsail from my lord Chauncelour
Declaring certayn causes which moved hym to stay the sealinge
of master prices patent for the offices of Secretory with the kinges
Counsail in wales / referring hym self nevertheles to such
answer as the Counsail here woold make vnto hym to be folowed
in the same /

The French ambassadour arryved at the courte to speke
with the kinges highnes and having audience proponed that by
Mater of the statutes for the avoyding of straungers avoyding
& payment of the subsidie; the mater of the passage broken at
Cowbridge the not deliuerye of modena wer contrary to the treatys
the treates myght be there right spoke whervnto the kinges highnes answered & euery poynt very princely
& rowndely / that the statutes wer no other then myght be made
without the offence of the treatyes & that the frenche kinges
Counsaillours that shewed their master otherwise ne could no skil
of treay4se & wer to be blamed & their master also for gyving
to lite lyght credence vnto them / and that as for the
brekyng of the passage at Cowbridge he tooched not
his Master grownde but vpon his owne made trenches
& ditches which he myght lawfully do & woold defend
the same if the french king woold go about to fill them
with no lesse force then they woold defend theyrs for all
their high bragges / and that although the french king tooke wm
small occasion wher non was yet his highnes pretermitted
many thinges wherin he had cause to think hym self well
handeled as in the deteynyng of the traytour that callith hym
self blanche rose (and in ded that was the very commencement
of thambassadours discourse with the king) and also for the
conswade which was kept from his highnes iniustely / &
wheras the ambassadour pressed the king to know wheder he
woolf have blanche Rose he answered that he forced not so
moch for his persone & for the vngentle handlyng of the frenche
king in the same / & no feder And when the ambassadour
obiected the not delyuery of modena / the king answerd
that he was ones delyuerd & thervpon by his masters ambassadour

sett at large and forasmoche as he was a mylanoys
when so euer the french king was Duke of Myllan (as yet
themperour was) they woold he delyuer modena / The
which Discourse was signified vnto the Counsail at london
From the Counsail beyng here / requyring the same to
conseider bothe the Treatyes and also the statutes / in such
sorte as they myght be able to answer both themperour and
the frenche king / & to aduertise the kinges highnes of their
opinions in the same /

at the Moore the xvijth of octobre
beyng present the forsayd Counsaillours

A lettere to my Lord Chauncelour declaring the kinges pleasir
touching the Sealing of master pryces patent

The king was aduertised from the depute of Calais
how that the frenche men of arde had made agayn
the passage at Cowbridge which before was by
the sayd depute at the kinges commaundement
defeated / and that the xiijth of the present the sayd
depute cause the sayd passage eftesones to be broken
The sayd depute also sent hither the copye of his message sent by guysnes pursu
ynsat to the Capitayn of arde & his answer thervpon and after
that the Capitayns lettere to the depute & his answer
to the same which wer sumwhat poygnant & pickyng
vpon bothe sydes /

Letteres vnder the Stampe to the Commissioners of Sewers in
the Countye of Sussix for the making again of the saltewater
and freshewater gates vpon the ryver of Lewes / which had
bene lately broken vp by certayn persones as yet vnknowen /

A lettere Vndre the Stampe to the Custumer of Sandwiche to
suffre corne & vitaill to passe to Calais /

A lettere to the president and the Counsaill in Wales for the
making of thindentures for the breede of horses & Mares
with Ryce ap Morys according to certain articles sent to hym
in the sayd lettere

A lettere to the Lord Admirall to aduertise themperours ambassadour
that the king had commaunded certain of his counsaill to
conferre the treatyes & statutes that thervpon they shuld
haue aunswer / of his questyons / of such thinges as he
and that furthermoore the king is contented vpon themperours
request to gyve licence to them of Dunkerke to bye and
cary ouer vncertain nombre of billetes so his highnes might
be furst j certified of the nombre / and that the same
wer reasonable /

Letteres wer brought From Master Wotton out of Cleves
declaring the ouertures made vnto hym by the Duke and his
Counsail tooching his letteres to king ferdinand & kinges ferdinand
Letteres vnto hym for sum good composicion to be taken betwene
hym & themperour for the duchie of goldnes / & that he master
wootton thought that the Duke having not but the sayd
Duke made no discourse vnto hym of the Duke henry of
he practise with hym for the mariage of the duchesse of
Myllayn requiting hym the sayd Cleves that in the meane season that
he the sayd duke did practise with themperour for that purpose he the sayd
Cleves he shuld not entre any condederacy with any other prynce
for the myght be an empechement to theffect of his practise
which discourse of thinges master wootton lerned of a privette
secrete frende / & woold not have the same disclosed / Item in
the sayd lettere of merecrointh that he had that the quene of
navarre in her letteres wrytten with her own hand to the Duke
of Cleves called hym her sone /

A lettere to the depute of Calais willing hym to defeate from tyme
to tyme that which the frenchmen shuld do for the passage at Cowbridge
having allwaye an eye that by any enterprise he woold make
if did not put in any hasard or dawnger any of the towne or
any other of the fortresses /

Letteres wer sent to master wallop aduertising hym of
the hole discourse that the kinges highnes did make
with the frenche ambassadour

Certayn Tyndall men of those that had bene owtlawes
arryve whose names do ensue

cam vp hither in the place & name of all such as had bene
owtlawes to submitt them selfes in such sorte to the kinges
mercy & grace as yt myght apper to the his Maieste that they wer
most repentant for their offences and wer deferred from makyng
their submission vntil such tyme as the kinges highnes cam
to wyndesour /

A proclamacion vndre the stampe & signet was made tooching
aswel the inhibicion aswel of londoners to resorte to the courte cum within the
courte gates vntil they had furst knoweledge of the kinges
pleasyr / and as also forbidding those that went from hens
to london to cum in to the courte & furthermore that no man shuld
breke vp in the kinges houses or his parkes any doore wyndow lock or
pale vpon payn of prisonement
