Memorandum that this present daye a lettere in forme of a certificate bearing date
of the xxvth of this present was deliured to the Lordes and others of the
kinges highnes pryvey Counsaill signed with the handes of the Lord
Matravers deputye of the towne and Marches of Calais of syr
Edwarde wotton knight Treasorer of the same and syr Edwarde
Kingeley Comptroller there the desyring that threscore x tonne
of bere viij wayes of chese / xxty fliches of bacon x barrels & dy
of butter & CC stockfishes provided and laden at london for the store and victailling
of the towne of Calais / might be transported to Calais
without empechement of the Custumers Comptrollers of the said
porte which certificat was signed with thandes of the Duke of
Norffolk the Lord priuey Seale the gret Chamberlain of
Englande and the gret Admirall of Englande and sent
to the Lord Chauncelour of England for thaccomplishement of ye same

Wheras syr Thomas Wharton and syr William Musgrave knight
and John Heron of chipchace wer appointed by a lettere from signed by the
kinges highnes / to po levye threscore horsemen and a Capitain
there vpon the borders and to sende the same to London

A lettere was sent from the Counsail to syr George Lawson
knight requyring him to pay vnto the said Capitan &
horsemen conducte money from their dwelling places
vnto London after the rate accustumed but wherof
he shuld haue allowaunce in his accomptes


At Dover the xxviijth day of Marche
beyng present the counsail that wer
assembled the day before


At fover the xxixth of marche
beyng present the forsayd Counsail

A lettere was written to Thomas spurway the kinges highnes receyvour of
the landes certayns atteynted landes in the west partes
requyring hym that wheras
to content & paye vnto iij iiij pyoners which the
wheras the lord admyral had having commission to leve in those the west partes &
to cause to be conducted to calais their CCCC pyncers
& to cause them to be transported to calais had did appoynted Antony harvy
Esquier for his assigne in that behalf a lettere was written
to Thomas Spurway the kinges highnes receyvour of
certain atteynted landes in those partes to content
& pay vnto the sayd pyoners theyr at the appoyntement of the sayd harvy conduct
money after the rate of a halpenney the myle
from theyr dwellyng places vnto the seeside


At dover the xxxth of marche
beyng present the forsayd Counsail

Collectors of the xth and xvth in the all places
