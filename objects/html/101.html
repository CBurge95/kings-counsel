Sir John Fawel of Asheburton prist appered before
the Counsaill and was examined vppon his othe
vppon certain interrogatoryes ministred vnto him of
the bill of complaynt put vpp by Philip Brayn of exceter
against Sir Thomas Denys knight the Quenes
Chauncelour

Letteres wer written to the Lord Dacre to apprehende
and kepe surely and in a secrete sorte oon James
Duglas of Parkhed scotishman fled a yere past
in to thenglishe borthers /

Like letteres wer written to sir Thomas Wharton and sir
william Musgrave with further commaundement to
apprehende other twoo scottes called Robyn Rotherford
and Andrew Bell /

Letteres wer written to sir Cuthbert Ratcliff, and
John Heron of Chipchace for thapprehension of the
sayd Rotherford and Bell

At Hamptoncourte the vjth of
Januarye being present
The Lord Chauncelour
The Duke of Norffolk
The Duke of Suffolk
The Lord priuey Seale

his request touching his cummyng over /
