The iiijth day of octobre At hull
the presence being the same of the
day before

sir Jhon Aytlond Mayour, sir wylliam knowlis, knight fawbury lawrence and wer
appoyntid to be orderors and oversears of the kinges workes
at hull

The same day wer declared to them the kinges grawntes to re
teyne requestes made by the towne

First an incorporation for theyre mariners with licence to pare
chasse for the same xli by yere

Seconde that they sholde be allowyd of xli and add money
wont to be payed to the shrif and now surcissed

Thyrd that theyre sute for forrayne bowght and forrayne
solde sholde be proclaymid

Fowrth that they sholde have a charter for orphans goodly
according to the ordre of london

Fifth that theyre sholde be a redresse taken for theyre marc
off danzig

They townesmen hav off hull have promised s that where
as the kinges grace prorposith for theyre welth and commodite
to debaurce aswell for the making of a brydge betweene them and
holdrnes as for other costly difenses for the savegarde of the
sayde towne intendith to debourse a great soom of moneye,
they for theyre parte wyll at theyre propre charges clense
and make deper theyre diches, and newly make theyre
slusys, and furnisshe syche gettes as shall be left open
for theyre commodite, with portcoullis etc and prouide a
curtayne of iron peces, and to entre with them of holdr
nes for soom concenient soom of money toward the making
of the bridge

And towching theyre chargis for in the sayde the kinges grace
plesir is that all the shire of hull shall be contriontonces

This day at the after none the king departid from hull
by see, to Thornton
