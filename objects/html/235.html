A Lettere was written to Antony Rous Esquyer Treasorer
of kinges Maiestes workes at Guisney to spende vp such wheate
and other thinges of the staple of victailes there as as could not
be kept and to retourne send the money therof to be made therof to James Sutton to provie other in stede of that the same / which
new provision he was appoynted to see it layed in their
before thendyng of his Maiestes cite guysnes workes there to the same to be be to remayn
for a staple / there in the place appoynted for the same

It was appointed that all the workmen Lately
working vpon Dublyn bulwerk at Calais shuld
be converted to the workes at Guisney except such
a nombre as shuld suffise for the making of the
Salue / out of the towne of Calais in to the saide
bulwerke and for the clensing of the dikes and for the
bringing on of the dove of erth betwene that and
Branchamps bulwerke and also for the making of the
Walle at Ruisbank from the towre next vnto the see
vnto the towre next vnto the towne the same thinges to be done before Michaelmas
and the rest of the Vanitinure of Dublyn bulwerke
shuld be for this yere furnished with Maundes It was
also appoynted that a convenient lodging shuld be
made for the Capitain of Ruisbank without plucking
Downe of the Rounde towre /
