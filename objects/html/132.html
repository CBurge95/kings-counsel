At Hamptoncourte the xxth day of February
being present
The Lord priuey Seale
The gret Chambrelain of Englande
The gret Admiral of Englande
The Treasorer of household
The Comptroler of household
The Master of the horses
The vicechambrelain
Sir Thomas Wriothesley Secretary

Wheras the bishop of Sares wrote a lettere vnto sir Thomas
Wryothesley knyght one of the kinges principall Secretaries
declaring vnto hym in the same that by way of complaint that
Darrell gentleman who had married the doughter of sir
william Essex knyght one of the kinges highnes Ordinary
Counsail had v of late without any licence entred in to the
parke of the sayd bishops called Ramesburye and there v
hunted his deere hauyng no lycence of hym for the same
Albeit the said fact beyng against the ordre of kinges Maiestes lawes
and statutes of the realme contrar and contrary to the
breche and disturbance of the good and quiet ordir
& rule of this realme was thought worthy of it
self of sharpe correction & punishement to the feareful
example of others that eles myght woold prevent attempte to
do the semblable yet the counsail aforsayd considering
having regard to the wisdome & degree of the sayd sir
william Essex and sumewhat also considering the yougth
of the sayd darrell wrote two severall letteres the
one to the sayd Darrell commaunding hym to repair
to his sayd father in law & to ensue such ordre and
determinacion as he shuld fo take with hym in this matter
the other lettere to the said sir William Essex declaring
vnto hym aswel the said complaynt against his the sayd darrel and
yet never as also the cause wherby they wer moved to remitt
the hering & ordir of the matter to hym and therfor requyred

hym to call the sayd darrel furthwith before hym and to
examyn hym gravely & substancially of the hole circumstances
of his sayd mysdemeanour and thervpon by hys good counsail
and aduertisementes to reduce hym to a better conformyte and
temperance and to advise hym to vse hym self aswell bothe towardes
the sayd bishop as and towardes alothers as shall apperteyne
further requyring the sayd sir William Essex to signifie vnto them
od his procedinges herin with the said Darrel in this said matter accordingly

Sundry letteres wer written to the commissioners for Saintuaryes
requyring them / to allot such places within the townes appoyned
for the said Sanctuaryes as might be convenient for xxty
Sainta Saintuary court & best moysum & incommodious for the
sayd ttwone and also where the Sainctuary men shall of necessity
whensoeuer they cum abroad be moost in the sight and eye
of honest men to thintent they shal not make priuey excourses
or resorte out by back and by waies secret wayes vnseen
of thinhabitauntes Aduertising the said Commissioners further
that the kinges highnes mynded not that this their limitacion
shalbe a perpetual establishement but only an inquisicion
for the moost convenyent place to be signified vnto his
maieste according to his highnes saide commission to thintent
that vpon consideracion of the same he might determyne
determyne his resolute pleasyr by his lettres patentes in such
wise as shalbe thought vnto his highnes moost expedyent
