Att westminster the xijth day off marche
being than present
The lorde Chawncellour
The lorde russell
Therle off hertford
The bisshop off winchester
The bisshop off westminster
The lorde saintJohn
Sir thomas cheyney
Sir John gage
Sir Antony brownse master off the horsses
Sir Antony wingfelde
Sir thomas wriothesly

One Tirrell seruant to master chawncellour off thaugmentations
aduertised by one parker that one Smith off the
garde and others mynded anone after ester to steale certeyne
horsses and to bring thesame in to a woodde nigh vnto london /
And being cownseilled allso by the sayde parker to consent to the
stealing off the sayde horsses owt off the sayde woodde from
the sayde smith / Hauing first recealed this matter
vnto master chawncellour This day revealed thesame to the
cownsell / by whome he was nott onely charged to kep the
thing secret, butt to do allso his best for the parfight trial
off the hole trewth thereoff For the whiche purpos assurance
was made vnto him he sholde incurre no dawnger for any
meane he showlde vse for the furtheraunce hereoff and farther
that he sholde be allowed off all his costes and expenses to be
employed hereabowght And finallye to haue suche a rewarde
as for his paynes and diligence in this behalff sholde
be thowght expedient


Att westminster the xiijth day off marche
being than present as
Tharchebisshop off canterbury
The lorde chawncellour
The lorde russell etc as the day before

Letteres war directed to the capitaynes off the kinges nauye
vpon the sees / that wheras one John bonde was appoynted
by the deputye off callais to prouide certeyne vittaylles for the
furniture off thesame / They sholde geve the sayde Jhon bonde
al teh ayde and assitence they cowlde for the sure convayance thereoff
accordinglye /
