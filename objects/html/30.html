Thomas Claye of the towne of Thorpe in the contye of Essex carpenter beyng browght before
the Counsail by
taken with stolen gooddes about was vpon examinacion comitted to
the warde of the Marshall /

It was enioyned to albert the Millener that he and
his felow shuld repaire to the lord Chauncelour with a lettere from
the Counsail and takyng with them the two letteres of denyzens which
wer supposed confessed by therin to have bene gotten out vnder the grete seale of england without
any warrant / and declare vnto the sayd Lord Chauncelour by sufficient
prove their innocencye tooching the s in the sayd mater /
