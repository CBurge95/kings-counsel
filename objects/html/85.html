Spensir the bisshopp of Norwiches Chauncelour and to
Thomas Godalue / gyving them thankes for thapprehension
of Thomas Whalpole being taken for the setting furth
of an epi sedycyous epistle of Melanctons and requiring
theim to sende the sayd Thomas Whalpole vp hither
And the other lettre was written to sir Gyles
Alyngton knight Sergeaunt Hynde Philip Parys
and Thomas Megges Esquyers to take a chaplayn of
the bishopp of Elyes called Foxforth or such a like
name and one Deryck a siruant of the sayd bishopp
accused for setters furth of the sayd epistle and to sirche
their chambres and to sende theim vp hither to the
Counsaill and in case that it appered certainly to them
that the bishopp was of Counsaill of the sam translacion
of the sayde Epistle they shuld alsoo syrche the
bishopps studye and charge him to appere before
the Counsaill incontynently

The forsayd Scotish knyght was answered
that in cace the his Master woold delyuer certain
traytours to the kinges Maieste fled out of England
whose names shuld wer deluered vnto they the
sayd knight the kin sayd the traytour before
named shuld be diligently sought for & delyuered
if the could be fownd / and as toching the licence
for geldinges & bowes & arrowes he shuld haue
such an answer at his retorne from themperour that
shuld be to his reasonable contentacion / and
so was dimissed to his lodging to kingston
beyng accompaygnied with sir Thomas arundel
knyght /

Wheras oon Watier Chaundeler of the cytye of
wynchestre exhibited to the kinges Maieste a bit of
complaynt against sir Thomas Wryothesley knight
oon of the kinges Maiestes twoo principal Secretaryes
accusyng the sayde master Secretarye to haue kept
from him by extorte power and by force certain
Manour called Sechingwoorth Cherk and Lee with
thappertenances in the countye of Southhampshire without paing
or gyving to the sayd Chaundeler any recompence
for the same And alledging aswel in the said
bil as also reaporting (as enformacion was cume)
many other slaunderous woordes by and against the
sayd master Secretary / the which bil of complaint
hath at sundry tymes before the said Counsail
bene debated and bothe the partyes indifferently herd
speke and saye what eyther of them could saye and
alledge in the maintenance & defence of his cause
Forasmuch as it appereth and is certain vnto
the sayd Counsail by the deposicion of sundry witnes
duely examyned by their othes that the sayd matier
of complayn touching the sayde bargain is false
vntrue and slanderous and that the sayde Chaundelour
hath doon (asmuch as in him Heth) to detracte
hyndre and diminishe aswel to the kinges Maieste
himself as to al others his Maiestes moost honorable
Counsail the honestye good name credite and
estimacion of the sayde sir Thomas
It is by the sute consent and mature deliberacion
of al the Counsail aforsayde ordeyned determined
and decreed that the sayd Chaundeler shal furst
for the declaracion of the sayde Master Secretary
honest procedynges and true dealing with him in
the bargayn of and for the sayd Manours confesse
that falsely & vntruely he hath slaundered the
sayde Master Secretarye and therfor in the
presence of the sayd Counsail shal aske
the sayd Master Secretary for quenes and after

pagebreak


at the cummyng of the sayd Chaundelour home in
to his cuntrey he the sayd Chaundelour shal in all
such places wheras he had at any tyme before
misreaported and spoken evil of the sayd master
Secretarye publishe and Declare that he had doon
and sayde amysse vntruely & slaunderously without
the desyrt of the sayd Master Secretarye
The furst parte of which decree the sayd Chaundelour
did furthwith accomplishe in the forsayde presence
confessed his offence against the sayd master Secretary
and asked him forgiuenes Reginald Williams
and John Milles of the towne of Southhampton being
present /
