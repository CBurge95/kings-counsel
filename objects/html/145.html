At Hamptoncourte the iijde day of Marche
beyng present
the lord privey Seale
The gret chamberlayn of England
The Gret Admyral of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Treasourer of household
The Master of the horses
The vicechamberlayn
Sir Rafe Sadleyr Secretary

The lord hastinges exhibited the deposicion in writing of Thomas
Dawes of Ildersley in the countye of Derbye taken by syr
John Villers of ben knyght concernyng sundry traitorous
words spoken by syr against the kinges Maieste by sir Robert
Moore prist parsonne of brodley in the sayd countye
and conceyled and Mainteyned by the said pristes a
woman called which the said prist kept
in his house as apperith b whervpon two severall
letteres wer written one to the said syr John Villers gyving
vnto hym thankes condigne for his paynes taken in the
said examinacion the and d desyring hym to aduertise the
Erle of Shrewsbury to whome the further examinacion
of the said Matter was committed of all such his procedinges
hering as myght condence to the declaracion of the
trowthe in the same / the other lettere was written to
the sayd Erle requyring hym on the kinges Maiestes
behaulf furthwith vpon the recept of the said lettere to cause
the sayd pryste and his woman in case they be yette at libertie to be apprehen
ded and commytted to sure and safe custodie and callyng vnto hym suche
Justices of the peax or other lerned in the Lawe as he shall thinke mete
by his discreacion not only to examyn straytly the sayde pryste and his
woman of the premisses but also to sende for the sayde depouet & for all
such other persones as he shall knowe canne depose in in the sayde treasons and
in suche wise to examen the same as the hole trothe of the sayde treason with
all suche as be accessaryes to the same / may be knowne accordyngly And
in cace the saude pryste and his woman canne be convynced either by their
owne confessyon or other due proue to haue offended in the sayde trasons
or any of them then to see suche ordre taken by the advise of the sayde Justyces
or others learned in the Lawe as they maye be endyted of the sayde treasons
and at the next Sessions there of Gaeole delyvery arraigned vpon the
same and thervppon suffre suche punishment of deathe as by the kinges Maiestes
Lawes is provyded in that behaulfe / Furthermore desyryng his him
Lordeship to calbefore him Luyston a gentleman of that sayde Countye
and substauncyally to examyn hym wheder that having the matter brought
before him he hathe handled the same so slenderly as the sayde bill of
deposycion dothe pupourte and to aduertyse the Counsayle what he shall fynde
