At Ampthil the xxjth day of September
beyng present
The gret chamberlayn of England
The gret Admyrall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Treasourer of household
The Master of the horses
Sir Thomas Wryothesley Secretary
The Chauncelour of thaugmentacions

To my lord of Surrey Letteres wer sent to my lord of Norfolk od the counsailes
advise howe he shuld knowe the opinions of the Iustices
spilman & Jeny or such others in those quarters beyng
Lerned in the Lawes or he shuld thinke metist
how the sayng Lawe did waye the saynges of John
Kynton / and thervpon to procede against hym as his
desertes did requyre /

Letteres wer sent to John Harington esquier that
he shuld send to the courte his neighbor that had bene
in Irland and whom he had reaported to the king
could depose against the Lord Leonard / and that
also putting in wryting all such convercacions as the
the sayd maters he shuld send the same subscribed
with his hand to the courte

Letteres were sent to my lord Chauncelour in the
fauour of to be custers Rotulores in
Glowcestre shyre /


At Ampthil the xxijth Daye of September
beyng present
The great Chamberlain of England
The gret Admirall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Tresaurer of the kinges household
The Master of the horses
The Secretary Master Wriothesley
The Chauncellour of Thaugmentacions

The kinges pleasir was signified to Master Daw sir John
Dawncye knyght that wheras he w had warned certayne
persones in the Isle of Wight out of their fermes wherof
they pretended to have leasses & betted had diuised the same
to others that mynded not to dwell vpon the same he
shuld suffre take ordre that the old tenantes myght enioye
their leasses vntil Mychaelmas circa a twelvemoneth
& that in the meane season the kinges highnes wold se
aduection taken in the matter /
