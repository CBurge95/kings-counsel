At Waltham the xjth of Maye
being present
The Archebishop of Canterbury
The Duke of Norffolk
The gret ChamberLain of Englande
The Erle of Hertforde
The bisshop of Duresme
The Treasorer of household
The Master of the horses
Sir Thomas Wriothesly Secretary
Sir Rauff Sadler Secretary

A lettere was written to syr George Lawson knight for the
payment of the wages to viij Bowyers and xiij Fletchers
whom the kinges highnes sent in to the North parties for
the reparacion of his Maiestes Bowes and arowes there
after the rate of xijd by the day to they ether of the twoo Master
bowyers and Fletchers / and to euery other of the bowyers
and Fletchers viij d by the daye begynning the ixth
day of this present of May and so contynually during all
their abode in those partes /


At Waltham the xijth day of Maye
the Counsail did not sitt bicause the
kinges highnes removed fromthens to
Grenewhich


The xiijth and xiiijth day of May the
Counsail was absent from Grenewhich
about the hering at Westminster of certain
matiers betwene the Murchauntes Clothyers
and Clothwourkers /
