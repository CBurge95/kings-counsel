At Ampthill the xxiiij of September
being present
The gret ChamberLain of England
The gret Admyrall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Tresorer of household
The Master of the horses
The Secretary Master Wriothesley
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Letteres wer sent to my Lord Chauncelour vndyr the Stampe
for the adiournement of the terme vntill Crastino maz

Letteres vnder the stampe wer sent to the president & counsaill
of wales / for the hering and determynyng of a complaint
put vp to the Counsaill here by the tenantes of Flyntshire
against Rogier Brereton Shiriff of the same shyre /

Letteres wer sent to Thomas Fulgham of Rysley in the county of Bedfordshire vndyr the stampe /
to appere personally vpon the receipt of the same /
byfore the kinges his counsaill attendaunt vpon his person
to aunswer vpon to the complaint of Huet of the
same towne and countye / to aunswer to such thinges as
at his cummyng shalbe obiected to him

It was declared to the Lady Poole the wief of sir Geffrey
Poole that the kinges highnes had pardoned her husband
of his imprisonment and had further commaundement that
the kinges pleasir was / her husbande shuld not cum in
to the courte vntill the kinges pleasir wer determyned
otherwise in that behaulf /

Thomas Smyth of Bedfordshire was sent for by Hugh Willoughby
Sergeaunt at Armes / to appere before the Counsaill
