A lettere was written to the lord warden desyring
hym that wheras thinhabitantes of Rye had taken
and imprisoned a certain man of Deapre in such sorte
as thowght they had had a lettere playn reprisall &
lettere of Mark (likes as be a lettere sent from the
frenche ambassadour to the lordes of the consail
apperith which lettere was also sent to the sayd lord
warden) he woold take such spedy redresse therin
as to Justice shuld apperteyne

Thomas Barnabe a siruant one of the kinges which
highnes siruantes which had commission certain thing &
in doyng for his Maieste in the parties of beyond
beyng retorned hither for the declaracion of his
procedinges in the same and doubting to be vexed
of sundry persons aswel of london as eles where
vppon private sutes had a supersedeas vnder the consailles
had to the Maiour of london etc Sherif aldermen & shyriffes
of london & to all other the kinges highnes offices not
only to permitt & hym to passe & repasse to & from the
counsail at al tymes vntil he wer discharged dimissed
from the same but also without any maner sute or troble
but also in case they had arrested or attached any
of his gooddes they shuld cause the same to be redelyuered
vnto hym accordingly

A commandement was dyrected from the Consail
to all maner the kinges the Maiour of Northampton
& to all other the kinges officers to ayde and
assiste Rafe Holfer & John Belson two of the
yemen of the garde for the prosenteng from place
to place & to take one gregory Myllet a
felon & to thentent they he myght bring the same be browght
before the sayd Counsail by the sayd two yemen of the
gard with all suche money wrying Jeweles & writinges
as shuld be fownd about hym at the tyme of
his apprehension /
