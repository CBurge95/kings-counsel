Vpon a contention betwene sir thomas wharton / and briskow
for a howse named the new hawle in the cowntye off Cumbrelande / Letters
wer deuised to the bisshop off carlile / sir Jhon lowther knight / dawlton
and thomas blanderhassett esquiers / to examine theyre claymes and rightes
and thereoff taduertise wyth diligence /

The commissioners off ireland appered wyth theyre bookes / and wer remited
tyll a nother day /

Att westminster the third off februarie the
presence being as the day before
except tharchebisshop off Canterbury

Whereas oon pasqual a strangier for his lewde behavour
to the kinges watche / and wordes spoken to the same to the delegation
off the kinges honour was commawnded by the cownsell to avoyde the
realm / and after the first day off february next ensuing nott to be
fownde wythin any the kinges dominions / Apon a supplication exhibited
to the lordes by the sayde pasqual / declaring a shippe to be on the
thamise whiche shortly sholde departe toward his natif contrey / desiring

pagebreak


that he might haue respite to tarry here tyll the departing off the same /
The lordes off the cownsell for that the tyme off the departure off the sayde shippe
was thowght to be vncertayne / resolued to length his tyme and to permitt and
suffre the sayde pasqual to continew wythin this realm oon hole month after
the date abousayde / And in cace the sayde shippe by that tyme sholde
nott be in a redines to departe / than the sayde paskall to ma declare
thesame and to make suit for a lenger tyme /
