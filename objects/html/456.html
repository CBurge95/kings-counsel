The mayour and the recordour off london wyth certeyne off
the aldreman exhibited vnto the cownsell conteyning certeyne
misdemeanoures and abueses abusese accustomably vsed wythin
the cite towching eating off flesshe in the lent / and
the braking off sondrye wyndowes in the night in diuerse
palces off the sayde cite / and the licentiows manner off
playours /

Aprill being than present
The lorde Chauncellour off Englond
The lorde russell lord privy seas seale

Sir Antony wingfelde vicechamberlain
Sir thomas wriothesley secretary

Therle of surrey being sent for tappere before the cownsell
was charged by the sayde presence aswell off eating off
flesshe as off a lewde and vnsemely manner of walking
in the night abowght the strates / and breaking wys wyth
stonebowes off certeyne wyndowes / And towching the eating
off flesshe he alleged a licence allbeitt he hadde nott so secretly
vsed thesame as apperteyned / And towching the stonebowes he
cowlde nott denye butt he hadde verye evyll don therin
submitting himsef himselff therefore to suche ponisshement
as sholde to them be thowght good / whereapon he was committed
to the fleet

Thesame days wer allso called Thomas Wiatt and
yong pikering and being charged wyth the sayde offenses
they confessed the first / alleging therefore theyre license butt
in the seconde towching the stonebowes they vtterlye stode in
denyall notwithstonding they wer commanded to sew shew the
trewth thereoff vpon theyre allegeance / wherevpon wiatt
was commanded to the counter and pikering to the porters
lodge /
