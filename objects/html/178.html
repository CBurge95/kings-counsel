Richard Bobynson and William Harrys syruantes to George
Paulet Esquyeer / and John Benet of Elveton in the county
of Southhampton / being brought before the Counsail for huntyng
by night in Elveton parke belonging to therle of hertforde
and confessyng the facte wer sent to wynchestre to prison
with a lettere to the Shiriff and Jaylour to receyve them /

Symon Branthwayt of St Katheryns Smyth who had
exhibited vnto the Counsaill against syr Christopher
Morys / Master knight Master of the kinges ordenance /
a certain bill of complain conteynyng certain poyntes
of vntrouth as he supposed / vsed of by the said syr Christopher
towardes the kinges Maieste / Forasmuch as the said Symon
ws not able to prove the said bill and that the same did appere
semed manifestly / to haue proceded rather precede of value them of any iust cause
was enioyned furst to aske the said syr Christopher forgyvenes
vppon his knees before the Counsail / which he did accomplish and
thervppon was committed to the porteres warde for his
further punishement /
