Robert Sowthwell Esquier and William peter Doctor of
Lawe two of the kinges ordinary Counsaillours made
report of their procedinges in thexaminacion of the burnyng
matter tooching the burnyng of the stack of woodde in Surrey
and sayd that they had fownd who burned the furst stack
but it coud not yet be proved who had burned the fo last

Master Horsey & Master
Jasper Horsey Stuard & Wymonde Carew receyvour to
the Lady anne of Cleves wer before the Counsail to
know the kynges pleasir tooching her removing & other
thinges conteyned diuided in articles conteyned in a stedule
remaynyng with Master Wryothesley

John Barkeley of Canterbury browght appered before the counsail with
such witnesses as he had to depose tooching the mater
opened by hy of treason opened by hym at the
Moore to the kinges Counsail them beyng there
the xijth of October and the matter beyng herd
and appering false & to have beene promoted by the sayd berkley only of
Malice Doctor peter was discharged & the sayd
barkeley commaunded to pay vnto John Meere of the
towne of Cambridge bedel his costes whom he had
caused to be sent for a witnes his cos tenne shillinges
for his costes & one sir James piers w of Cambridge
aforsayd prist whiche vpon whose mowth vpon whose
report the said barklye grownded his accusacion was
committed to the colehowse /

George Whelpeley & Elys brooke wer examined of

comptrollers & sircheours and for such of the articles
as they can could not then prove (the which be noted with treys
vpon their heddes they hand had a further tyme granted
vnto them / at which tyme Gaywood vndersircheour
in essex w/ beyng specially accused by the for sayd parties
