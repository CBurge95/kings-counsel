Nicolas Wentworth of Lyuingstone in the county of
Northumbria gentleman exhibited a supplicacion aswell against syr
William poyner of the said towne Yeoman accusing him
to be a procurer of periurye and a gret embracer and
maynteyner of brabling matiers and sutes as also
agaynst william Bosymer Henry Bosymer and their wiefes
and John Bosymer single woman of the said towne / Layng to
their charge / that vpon Mary Magdalens day and evyn
Last past they called him openly Traytour wherupon
the said poyner being convinced by the deposition of sundry
witnesses to be gilty of the saide crymes was committed to
the porters warde / and for his further punishement adiudged
to be set vpon the pillory at Northampton warwyke Stanforde
Oxford and Alesbury at four severall markes dayes
with a paper written in gret letteres Declaring the cause
of his punishement And the said Bosymers and their
wiefes being also examined whither they had at the
forsaide tymes called the said Wentworth traytour
and denyeng it sayng that they neuer knew no treason by
him nor never did so call him wer neuertheles convinced
by sundry witnesses to haue spoken the said slanderos
woordes against the said Wentworth submitted
themselfes wherupon the Counsail considering
that the saide Rosymers had spoken the said woordes

rather in a fume and a dronkenship at such tyme

William lucye of chorlecote in the countye of warwike gentleman beyng
sent for by a lettere from the counsail as apperith before in the processe of the
xvth day of this present moneth For that appered and answered
that he had never made graunt by mowth to to Thomas Audrey
the kinges Maiestes gentleman herbenger of the ferme of the lordship of
Haversham in the countye of buckinghamshire as the sayd Oldney had before
alledged and yet nevertheles forasmocheas the sayd Oldney
was there present with witnesse to haue proved that they sayd lucy
had promised by mowth never to put hym owt of the sayd ferme
during his lief and the Counsail moved hym the sayd lucey to advise hym self
well on the matter and to consider that although the Disposicion
of the ferme ha dbene clerly in his hand yet seyng the kinges
Maieste had written so so ernestly in fauorably for gently vnto hym
in the fauour and preferrement of the sayd Oldnay it wer shold be
well done he if he woold of hym wherof to satisfie his Maiestes request & to recomp
ense his mais waif Richard ylshawe aforsayd sum otherway which there frankly he promissed
to do & sayd that he had entreated his man was contented to surrender
his sayd leasse & shuld bring it the morow aftre to northampton
& thervppon he wold make a new leasse to the sayd oldnay
