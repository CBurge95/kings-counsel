A lettere was written to the bailif of St albons to
set at libertie vppon sureties to be furthcummyng before
the counsail at all tymes within betwene this & Mychaelmas
a certain woman which was by a former lettere from
the counsail to the sayd bailif committed to ward for speking
vppon a light reaport certain folish woordes the
author wherof she could not declare /

Wheras Marten balkeskye scot had rased a saufcon
duct of the graunted by the king highnes in anno xxij
and the was apprehended vppon the same the president
of the and counsail and in the north doubting to what offences
the sayd fact did extend by the law supposing it rather not treason thentreat and thervpon
desyring to be aduertised from hens aswel how they
shuld procede against the sayd scot as against a
woman that was deteyned in prison for speking
of folish lygt woordes sownding to treason wer
answered that foras tooching the first case that
forasmocheas the kinges kinges Maieste consulting with certain
of his lerned consail here was answered that in their
opinions the same appered to be rather treason then
otherwise his woold cold not resolutely determyne
pleasyr therin vntill that the next terme that he had
the opinions of his iudges which woold not
be before the terme next terme / & therfor signified
vnto the sayd president & counsail they shuld in
the newe season deteyne in prison the sayd scot
& as tooching the woman if sufficient the matter
obiected against her cold be proved by sufficient
profes to procede therafter by the ordre of the law
if otherwise there to take such wayes for her
delyerance as they myght savefully do /

Sir John mychel prist parson of Racton in the countye of
sussex beyng accused by syr Robert Sandwiche prist chaplayn
to syr geoffrey poole knyght to haue spoken certain haynous and
traitourous woordes against the kinges Maieste viz that the king
and the said woordes in the vowed by the sayd syr Robert
to the said syr John face to face was to committed to the
tower to remayn their vppon a further triall / and the
said syr Robert bownd by recognisance as folowyth

The condicion of the recognisaunce is that if the said
syr Robert do not departe out of London except it contynew
& abyde at london or within and not departe from thens without
licence of the kinges counsail bewtene this and the feast of St and not
gyving knowledge to the clerk of the counsail in what place he dothe make his abode be furthcummyng and do appere before the sayd counsail at all tyme and
tymes vppon a convenient warnyng before the sayd
counsail at any tyme ebfore the sayd feast and if
also the sayd syr Robert do not wryte aduertise or
gyve knoweledge to the syr geoffrey poole knight
of his examinacion or deposicion in his matter directly
nor indirectly by word wryting or otherwise nor
receyve any woord lettere or other aduertisement from
the sayd syr geoffray but that he shuld do furthwith open
& disclose the same to the sayd counsail that then etc.
or eles etc.
