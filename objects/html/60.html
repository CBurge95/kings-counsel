Letteres A lettere wer written to the deputye of Calais declaring
the receipt of his letteres with the copyes of the Capitain of
Ardes letteres / and of his aunswers to the same / and willing
him to followe thordre for Cowbridge prescribed vnto him
in the kinges formalle letteres further signifiing vnto him that
vpon an aunswer of Thomas Fowler the Garrysons wages
for the which he wrote / shuld be payed accordingly
And that in the meane season shuld cawse Thomas Fowler
to paye vnto such of the garason as shuld be thought mete
their wages with such money as was due by the Staplers
for their Last shipping /

A lettere to Thomas Fowler / to sende hither a time estimate
aswel of such money as was due by the Staplers and as
his brother left behinde him as alsoo any of the garison
to apye vnto such of the garyson as the Deputye &
Treasorer shuld appoynt / their wages of such money
as is due by the Staplers /

A warraunt vndir the Stampe to the Mayour and
felloweship of the Staplers /

A lettere The Archebishop of Canterbury was requyred
by a lettere to sende doctor Bengen to the Towre of London

A lettere to the Stephen the Almayn to repayre to the courte
