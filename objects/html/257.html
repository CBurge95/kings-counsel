Jhon Jhonson was Chosen mayour of hull the day befour
with a greate contention this day cam before the cownsell and
ther shewyd certayne reasons whye it behouid him nott that
yere to be mayour, specially for that a great tyme off
the yere he sholde necessaryly be absent whereapon notwith
standing that the day of Charles was past yett for that
tyme only it was decreid that this election sholde be voyde
and that they sholde ageyne assemble themselffes to a new
whiche decre declarid by the clarcke of the cownsell in
theyre comming hows they proceydyd to a new election
and chose for mayour a Master Eylonde knight

The same day cam letters from Sir thomas wharton
conteyning thexcuse of the lorde maxwell towching a rade
made late in to thenglisshe borderes

A priuye seale was decreid to be sent owt for Sir nicholas poyntz
apon the complaynt of my lady barcley
