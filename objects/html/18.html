At Grafton the seconde day of Septembre
being present
The great Chamberlain of England
The gret Admirall of England
The Bishop of Duresme
The Treasurer of the kinges household
The Master of the kinges horses
The Secretary Master Wriothesley
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

Jone Newman was eftsones before the Counsaill examined
of her deposicion touching the sainges of Lyons and reaported
the same self sainges that she did before /

Antony Cope gentleman and sir John Pytt vicare of
Bambery were togiders face to face before the counsaill
and the sayde vicare denyed thaccusacions to be trew
and eftsones repeted the same tale that he put in
before in writing / and after the aunswer of Cope
ti the same which did affirme his accusacion put in
wryting against the prist and had witnes redy
for the confirmacion of the same / the Counsail
considering Lack of discrecion in the Vicare /
bounde him and a suertie with him in a recognisaunce
as foloweth

And thervpon Anthony Cope being called a parte had
honest commendacions of the counsaill for his openyng of the
sayde matier with an honest advise to bestow his lernyng
in place and tyme convenyent

Master Treasorer and the Master of the horse went from the
Counsaill to the king for the knoweledge of his pleasir
touching the matiers of the heron and other matiers
and brough aunswer that Jone Newman Regnald &
and all the brethern of Giles Heron except the
bastard brother shuld be dispeched with honest reward
for their chardges cummyng & going And that alsoo
letteres shuld be written to Master Sadler his highnes
Secretarye in the fauour of Christopher and John Heron
his siruantes And that the bastard shuld be still reteined
in warde / and further to be examyned vpon the
practise of Necromancye

A Lettere to my Lord Chauncelur cndir the stampe for the
sending of a Commission to the counsaill wherby in matiers
touching the king they and euery of them shuld have
authorytye to take recognisaunce of such as appere
before theim and furthermoore to make a saveconduict
at the request of the kinges of Scottes letteres which wer
sent to him after the forme vsed and accustumed
Lye had alsoo in the same lettere commaundement to sende
owt a Commission to Calais for thenquyrye of the vj
articles and certain detestable heresies maynteined
by one Thomas Dynton of Calais sent also to him enclosed
in the sayd lettere

An other lettere was sent vnto him for the calling in again
of the commission for the Subsidye in Oxfordshire &
to make a new insirting in the same sir John Williams
knight /

A lettere was sent to the warden of the Flete for the
delyverye of Christopher and henry herons fo somers
their without taking any fees of them /

A lettere was sent to my Lord of Cantorbury tappoint
a Comissary to be the sworn in the conmission for
thenquyrye of the vj articles at Calais

A lettere was sent to Master Sadler the kinges Secretarye by ye
kinges speciall commandement in the fauour of Christopher of & John
Heron his siruantes to kepe the same still in siruice notwithstanding
this their examinacion which was to be noted rather a tryall of
their trueth & a purgacion of theim from all suspicion thenne
any ignomynie or shame /
