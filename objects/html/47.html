Letteres wer written to the Lord Chauncelour aswell for the
dispeche of John ap Rices patent of the office of Secretary
in Wales as alsoo for the sendyng hither of an abbreuiate
of all the offices and fees with the Names of them that
haue them at this present in the Chauncery kinges
Benche or commyn place / And furthermore he was
requyred to send an iniunction at the sute of william
Willoughby of Orford in Suffolk vnto the tenantes of
the sayde Orforde / that they shuld suffre the said
Willoughby to enioye his inheritaunce in Orford &
Sudborne / vnto such tyme as the variaunce betwene
them for the same wer determined before the counsaill in the sterre chambre /

A lettere was sent to the Deputye Treasorer and Comptroller
of Calais to deliure the possession of the house &
gooddes of the Lady Banestre decessed which they
had ceased to the kinges vse / vnto Banestre
her sonne in Law for the vse of himself and others
that had right to the same / byndyng him in a
sufficient somme of money for the fourth cummyng
of the sayd house and gooddes in case that any
thing within a twelfmoneth canne be alledged
for the king wherby his highnes shuld haue
interest in the sayd house and gooddes

A lettere was sent to Master Pate declaring vnto
him that notwithstanding the cancred & traitorous
lettere that hylyares sent vnto him & his chaplain
wherby sum suspicion might haue bene concerned
the kinges highnes did conceyve right good opinion
of the trouth and fidelity both of himself & of his
chapelain towardes his Maieste wherin he might
a without being dismayed being repose himself
assuredly

A lettere was sent to diuerse of the lordes of the
pryvey counsaill being at London in commission for
the subsydy aduertising them of the kinges determinacion
for the sending for of the Lady Kempe & Masteres
vnto them to London and no further / and vpon
conformyty to satisfie the kinges pleasir to sendyng
them back imedyately to their owne houses for the

setting of an order and staye in the same/ tappointe
to repayre assone as they might conveniently thother to
the court wheras they shuld be furt being further
aduertised of the kinges pleasir in that behaulf
ye shuld furthwith departe fromthens to the plae
appointed for their abode

Four severall letteres wer written to the Master of the
wardes The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions and
tenthes and to the generall Surveyours for the sendyng
hither of an abbreuyate of all the offices and fees
with the Names of them that haue then at this
present within the Limittes of their severall offices
