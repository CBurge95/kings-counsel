At Westminster the xxvijth of June
being present /
The Lord Chauncelour
The gret ChamberLain of Englande
The Erle of hertford
The gret Admiral of Englande
The Vicechamberlain
Sir Rauf Sadleyr Secretary
The Chauncelour of the tenthes augmentacions
The Chauncelour the Tenthes /

A lettere was sent to syr Richard Bulkeley knight
the deputy Chamberlain of northwales requyring
him not onely to make restitucion here at thappointement
of te Counsail of a ship which had bene belonging sum
tyme to certain pyrates called Palmer and Carter
orels to signifye vnto them by his lettere a sufficient
discharge in that behaulf but also to cause all such
within the Limites of His office as bought any of the gooddes
which the saide pyrates robbed about fyve yeres past
vpon the cost of St Dauides from Bernard de Bernarde
and Peter Tylley Bryttons / to make restitucion
of the value of the same vnto his handes and he to sende
the same vp with the names of the partyes the sommes of
money receyved of them and the specialtyes of the thinges
which euery man bought And in cace any such of the
Byers as had any gret quantity of the said spoyle
shall refuse to repaye the value of the same
as aforsaide then to charge them so refusing to appere
furthwith before the kinges Counsail attendant vpon
his person / to make further answer then as
shal apperteyn /
