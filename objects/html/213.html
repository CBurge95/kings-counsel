A lettere was written to the Chauncelour of the Tenthes to
Delyver vnto the Lady Dacres of the South the somme of
fyfty poundes sterling /

A lettere was written to Master Sackfeld Shirif of Sussex
to deliure vnto the L said Lady Dacres aswell her hed all her apparail
of veluet saten perles stones or goldsmythes worke as also all pertayning aswel her hed other
her apparaill perteynyng as to to the rest of her body /


At Envile the thirde day of
July being present the same that was the
day before /

A lettere was sent to Master Chauncelour of the tenthes to cause
a dymy fowcre of Leadd to be deliured to Bernardyne
de Valoys / and a like lettere to the Master of thordenance /
to deliure quarterly vnto him a barell of saltpeter to
make powder and pellettes for theyr vse whom the
kinges Maieste had appointed him to teache to shote in a
gonne /

A Direction was taken by the Kinges Maiestes appointement
that Stephen the Almayn / shuld haue the Survey
and rite of the workes at Carlisle alone / and that
Thomas Gower shuld no loenger haue to do there but
medle onely with the workes at Berwyke And further
it was ordeyned that the bishop of Carlisle shuld
be as it wer Treasorer of the workes at Carlisle
and haue a clerc allowed him with the wages of viij d
by the day And that syr Thomas Wharton and
Eswarde Eglenby shuld be Comptrollers of the said
workes and be present at the leest one of them two with the
bishop Clerc at euery pay Daye / And wheras the
said Stephen had aswell gyuen certain enformacion
touchig thavauncement of the kinges workes there as
also made complain against syr Thomas wentworth

pagebreak


Capitayn of Carlisle for his evill vsing of the kinges
working there it was ordeyned that the said bishop
syr Thomas Wharton and Edward Egenby shuld examyn
the same and therupon aduertise the kinges highnes
and for those purposes a lettere was sent to the said bishop
syr Thomas Wharton and Edwarde Egleby / and
an other lettere to the saide capitain / to vse himself more
temperatly /

Vpon aduertysement from the Lord Chauncelour of
thattaindre of David Gunston Tyrell / and Robert Harvy
prist Commissary of Calais A lettere was written vnto him
signifying the kinges pleasyr that Tyrell vpon consideracion
shuld be repryed an that Gunston shuld suffre at London
according to his Judgement and the Commissary also
to suffre there And wheras the kinges
highnes had knoweledge from that Fraunces Haull whom he
had sent to Calais of Late with a plate for certain purposes Lay
sik on this syde Cantorbury hurt of a fall an other lettere was

sent addressed to the lord Chauncelour not only to send one for the sayd plate
to fraunch hand or aswel to cause a phisician and a surgeon
to repayr vnto hym for his comfort and relief as also
to send vnto hym for the sayd plate in case he wer not
able to r procede to Calais hymself for thaccomplishment

the chaunce of haul & the kinges pleasyr tooching
the retorne of the platt /
