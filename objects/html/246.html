At Cawood the vth of September
being the same that wer the
Day before

Sir Bryan tuke was requyred to deliure
in prest to the lord lady william Howardes wief
the kinges Amb to the lord william Howard
the Kinges Maiestes Ambassadour in Fraunce
the somme of CCxxiiijli for thre monethes dyetes before
hande to the vse of the sayd lord william over
and above such dyetes as he had receyved by two
other former warrauntes

The deputy and Counsail of Calais wer commaunded
by a lettere vndir the Stampe that all the kinges
affayres there shuld be treated among them all
and all their handes to be subscribed to all such
letteres as they shuld writte over to the king or the
Counsail of any matters to be passed their / and
in cace any of the Counsail shuld be absent vpon
sum reasonable occasion the rest to signify the same
in their lettere and if it shuld fortune any of them
to vary in opinion and therfor woold not subscribe
his Name the same to signify by his pryvate
lettere the cause that moveth hym to varrye
