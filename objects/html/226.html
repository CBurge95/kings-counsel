Wheras there is a variaunce depending before the kinges highnes
Counsaill in the Starre chambre betwene syr william a parr knight
keper of the kinges parke and warren of Molton in the County of Northampton
and the kinges tenantes of Molton and kingesthorpe and other mens tenantes
borderars vpon the saide parke and warren the saide keper alledging that
the Lymites and bondes of the said warren do and ought to extende
in as Large circuite and length as it is vsed in Molton kingesthorp
pyseforde and Boughton feldes at this present . and the tenantes contending
sum of them that the warren is vsed in a more Large and extreme
extente / and compasse thenne it ought to be / and sum of them plainly
affirming that there ought to be no warren there at all certain of the
Lordes and others of the priuey Counsaill haue made perambulacion
of the said warren and therupon calling before them the said syr William
a parr for his owne parte and also Richard Humfrey the bailliffes of
kingesthorpe Sir honksborough of Naysbye old Morgan and
sundry others for themselfes / and in the Name of all the tenantes of the
Towneships aforsaide / haue not onely receyved a promesse of them
that they shall stande to and abyde such ordre as the said Lordes
and others will determyne in this mattier but also charged them
all in the kinges Maiestes Name aswell to kepe the peax out
to an other and to surceasse from such sutes as one of them haue
commenced against an other asalso to vse themselfes the keper against toward
the tenantes and the tenantes toward the keper and the gamre in such hoenst
and indifferent facion as there ryse no new matyer of contencion betwene
them before the detirminacion of the forsaide controversie For the
more expedicion wherof and to thintent the same may be defined this
Michaelmas time next ensuyng the Lordes before named and others
of the Counsail haue gyven commission to syr Edward Montague

pagebreak


knight chief Justice of Englande syr John Williams knight Tresorer
of the kinges Justices syr william Newnam knight Shirif of the forsaide
Shyre and Robert Tyrwet Esquyer one of thordinary Esqers
for the body to the kinges Maieste thre or two of them at the leest
to examyn and enquyre by all such reasonable meanes as they
canne devise aswell of the right of the saide warren as of
the perambulacion Limites and vse of the same And furthermore
to perswade and reduce both the saide partyes to an equall and
indifferent agreement Referring nevertheles the perfaict
conclusion of the same to thordre of the saide Counsaill
And what the said Commissioners shal haue done both in this
parte and in all other their procedinges touching the hole matier
aforsayde they by appointed to make reaport to the Counsaill
the begynning of the next terme in the starre chamber crastino anare next ensuing to thintent such a small
determinacion may be made therin as shall apperteyn

Nycolas Browne of pakyngton in the countye of
Warwik yeman was eftesones brought before the counsail
and examyned who made the books which he had presented
at St albons to the Kinges maiestes wher vnto he answered
constantly that hys self was thautor of the books and maner man of
had seene it it but hym selfe eles but but as tooching the
contentes of the booke he said that he woold not defende
but submitt hys iudgement therin to the kinges maieste whervpon
the counsail by forasmoche the kinges highnes speciall commaundement (with whom
whoese hys clemencye considering of his clemencye that the felo men had bene sundry
alredy punished in prison for his presumption & foly had willed
then his counsail before in cace the sayd browne woold shew hym seld repent
yt they shuld to take hys submission & so to let hym go
gave the sayd browne & very sharpe admonicion to be war
herafter of any such attemptate & to take hede that

pagebreak


they perceyving hym vppon his said confession to be very repentant & sory that he had
presumed so folishly to medle in matters of so gret weight
to attempte such a folish and nawghty acte and promising
never after to do the semblable but to renowne and forsake
all such lewd & nawghty matters as wer conteyned
in the said book & tooching the same to submitte hymself
to the mercye & iudgement of the kinges Maieste liek as more
playnly appertyth by a bill of submission written and
subscribed with his owne hand / did gyve hym a wry sharpe
monicion and so dimisse byn and warnyng to vse hymself
herafter in all his doynges & spekes conformably to the good
kinges maiestes lawes of and statutes of the and so byndyng hym
to bring in to syr Thomas Wriothesley Knyght oone of
the Kinges maiestes two principall secretaries after the progresse all such
other booke Mynutes as he had drawen of the sayd bookes
& all such other bookes as he had tooching any like matters
they dimissed hym & sett hym at libertye

then keper of the sayd wooddes wherin the said tenantes had commyn for the pasture of theyr catel makyng certain suggestion to the
kinges Maieste to haue one quarter of the sayd wooddes enrailled
from the commyn of the sayd tenantes and obteynyng his said sute
dyd enraylle vnder the colour of one quarter there enrayle
thre quarters of the sayd woodde to the gret enpouershing
of the sayd tenantes The sayd James hide doth not only
kepe the said thre quarters enrailled certain wheras the
but also hath hedged the same otherwise then wo hath
bene so before his tyme vsed so as the hogges calues
& shepe of the sayd tenantes which canne not entre into
the said quarters to side as they wer wont to do / complayning

further that the said keper restraynyth them of theyr accustumed foote path

Wheras the kinges Maieste being at this tyme in his
progresse towardes his highnes cyty of York myndeth
to passe thorough his town of Stamforde Being
thaldreman and thinhabitauntes therof warned and demaunded
of their libertyes there hath arrisen matyer of contencion
touching the same betwene the shiriff of Northamptonshire
Thalderman of the said towne and the baillif of the
libertyes of Peterborough that is to say how farre euery
of the libertyes aforsaid do extende and shuld be
vsed / and how euery of the said Shirif Baillif of liberties
and Alderman shall gyve their attendaunce on his Maieste
passing through the parishe of St Marten on this side
the bridge standing vpon the water of Weylande
and as it shuld appere in the county of Northhamptonshire

pagebreak


the said shiriff clayming to cary the white rodde before
the kinges Maieste vntill the fote of the saide bridge on this
syde the said water alledging that neyther thalderman of
the Towne nor the Baillif of the libertyes ought to
bear any man or other thing before the kinges Maieste
th till his highnes arryvall at the said place of
the bridge / The baillif of the libertyes clayming thoffice
of the saide shiriff within the precincte of the saide libertyes that
is from thentre of in to St Martyns parishe vnto the saide place
of the bridge tapperteyn vnto him onely and not to the shirif
of the Shire Thalderman of the saide town clayming of
of right to mete the kinges Maieste out of the hole towne / and
before his arryvall in the said St Martyns parishe and to
cary his Maste before his Maieste through the said paryshe of
St Martyns and so thorough the rest of the towne vnto thende
of the precincte of the saide towne in Lincolbshire The kinges
Maiesty and the Lordes and others of his Maiestes priuey
Counsail attending vpon his moost royall personne
considering that the shirif is his Maiestyes principall &
ordynary offycer for the County of Northamptonshire aforsaid
and that eyther the baillif of the libertyes hath yet
proved that at any tyme heretofore the baillif for the
tyme being hath caryed before the kinges Maiesty
any rodd nor yet the saide Alderman can shew any
sufficient graunte why he shuld cary the Maste before
the kinges Maiesty from and throught the place he claymeth
hath ordeyned and decreed that for this tyme that from this
tyme onely the shirif shall cary before his Maiesty
the white rodde through the said parishe of St Martens
vnto the middes of the said bridge and no further / and at
the parte of the bridge on thother side thalderman to mete
his Maieste with his Maste and so tattende vpon his highnes
vnto the furthest precincte of the said towne on the further
syde of the water / And the said baillif of the libertyes
for this present to surcease and in no wise to entremedle which
sayd ordre the kinges Maieste and his Counsail well and
ordeyn shall take place for thavoyding onely of confusion at
this tyme in attendaunce vpon his Maiesty and in no to
be taken or interpreted herafter eyther to the periudice of any of

pagebreak


the titles of the said Alderman and Bailliff which
any of them shalbe able herafter to shewe or declare for
the Mayntenance of their claymes touching the carying
of the said Maste or Rodd or of any other matyer which
they shall herafter alledge being mayntenable or to thenlarging
of thauthority of the shiriff otherwise then herafter
shalbe Determyned / Provided alway that notwith
standing the saide ordre all & every thinhabitauntes
of the said parishe of St Martyns / shalbe and contynue
contribucions and pay towarde the xvth knightes & burgesses fees
and all other paymentes and do all and euery such other
thing and thinges with thalderman and burgesses of the
said towne of Stamforde as the said inhabitauntes
of the said parishe of St Martens / haue bene vsed
to pay and do heretofore before the making of the saide
Decree / Any thing in the same conteined to the
contrary in any wise notwithstandyng /
