It was ordeyned that Marten Comptroller of the kinges
workes shuld with all diligence sende over to Calais CCC
Labourers mete for the casting of ditches and for that purpose
letteres wer written aswel to the said Marten to repayre
to the lordes at London and to receyve of them conducte
money for the said Labourers / and to sende sum honest
Man to haue the conducte of them over to Calais
as also to the sayde Lordes to delyver vnto the said
Marten such a somme of money as they by their discrecions
shuld think sufficient for the transportacion of the same
wherof they shuld be satisfied again by a warraunt
by a warraunt to be sent to them from hens vpon aduertisement
from them of the certaintye of the somme / And at this
present there was sent aswel a lettere to the Sirveyour of the
workes at Calais to se the said Labourers bestowed ymmediately
vpon there arryval there vpon the casting of ditches in such
places and in such sorte and maner as Rogers the Master Mason
of the Workes at Guisney shuld declare vnto him on the
kinges Maiestes behaulf as also a warraunt Vndir a
stampe addressed to Anthony Rous Esquyer Treasorer
of the Workes at Guisney for the payment of such wages
vnto the said Labourers from the day of their arrival
there by the discrecion of the Sirveyours and at such
Dayes as thother workmen there be accustumed to
be payed /
