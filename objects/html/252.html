September being present
The Duke of Norffolk
The Duke of Suffolk
The lord privey Seale
The gret Chamberlain of England
The gret Admirall of England
The bishopp of Duresme
The The Treasourer of household

The Master of the horses
The Vicechamberlain
Sir thomas Wriothesley Secretary
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

A proclamacion was made that of any maner performe who soever
dwelling in these partes founde himself gryeved for lack
of Iustice ministred vnto him / eyther by the kinges Maiestes Counsail
resident now here in this cyty of york resident in this present or by any other which his
Maieste had put in trust hertofore for that purpose / his highnes
pleasyr was that in cace they and protest sayd this self grevd could iustely prove any iniustice steg st
to haue bene done vnto hym them there they if the same parte shuld haue free accesse
to his the Maieste & his Counsail attendant vpon his person
to declare here his said grief and thervpon receyve shall have
indifferent iustice ministred vnto them right at thande
of his Maieste with fauourable audience during his graces abode
in these partyes

Robert Richardson a scottishe Frere was apprehended and eftsones brought
before the Counsail who being examyned and nothing founde
by him wherfor he shuld be stayed any lenger was dimissed
with a lettere of Saufconduct to passe freely through this Realme
in to Scotlande
