Twoo other placardes of the same effect passed vndir
the Stampe to Preston

A lettere was sent to the Lord Chamberlain to Guisnes
signifying vnto him the kinges pleasyr was that he
shuld imedyatly Discharge his Crew of Cvj men which
he led ouer with him and that Thomas Fowler had
commaundement to see therin paied vntill the iijde day
of Septembre next ensuyng

The Mayour of the Staplers was commaunded by a lettere
and iij or iiij of the moost auncient of the Staplers
in the name of the rest to be at Ampthill the viijth
daye of Septembre next / and to bring with them such
pactes as passed Betwene the king and theim concerning
their shipping of wolles and felles to Calais /

The Deputie of Calais was aduertised from the counsaill
of their proceding here with Thomas Fouler and of
the Dischardge of the Lord Chambrelains Crew
of Cvj men /

The Lord priuie Seale was aunswered to the foure
poyntes of his Last lettere on the kinges behaulf as foloweth
Furst that a proclamacion shuld be sent furth out of
hande / Wherby Straungers borne / Being at this
present in the fee wages hyre ordynary dyes or retinue
of the king the Quene / the prynce the kynges other
children or the Lady Anne of Cleves be at liberty
to kepe such houses chambers and Seruantes as they
haue doon without any daungier of the Last acte or any
other actes And that thexecucion of the said Last act
against other straungiers now enhabitauntes thenne
next As touching the seconde point Fouleers articles
with the aunsweres totted vpon euery article wer sent
vnto him / Thirdly the king was contented that
he shuld enioyne the priste of Wyndesour to resyde
vpon his benifice / and to giue him a lesson to temperate
his tongue / hereafter vpon aduenture of further
punishement/ And finally concerning the prisoner
