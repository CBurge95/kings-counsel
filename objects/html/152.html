A lettere was written to sir william Essex knyght to send
vppe b to the counsail one John Ward prisoner within the castle
of Oxford which to be further examined of certain treasons wherof
which wer deposed aga by sundry wittnesses to haue bene
committed & done by hym

A lettere was sent to william popeley to repayr vp hither
to the Counsail vpon the recept of the same lettere and
to bring with hym all such wrytinges as he had in his
keping concernyng the matter of a certain bretayne
which complayned hym self to haue had his shippe spoyled
about St. Dauides in Wales /

And that this present daye a lettere in forme of a certificat bearing date of
the vth of this present was delyved to the Lordes and others of the kinges
highnes priuey Counsaill signed with the handes of the lord Matravers
Deputy of the Towne and Marches od Calais of syr Edward Wootton
knight treasorer of the same and william Sympson one of the kinges
Counsaill there desyring that CCC quarters of Malt which they had
caused to be provided here within the Realme for the furniture of
the said towne & Marches / might be lade at London and transported
to Calais without empechement of the custumer & comptroler of the said port
which certificate was signed with thandes of the Duke of Suffolk the lord
pryvey the gret Chamberlain of England The Vicechamberlain and syr
Thomas wryothesley knight Secretary & sent to the Lord Chauncelour
of England for thaccomplishment of the same /
