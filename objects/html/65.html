Letteres wer written to the comptroller of Calais signifyng
vnto hym that the king highnes tooke in god parte his
serious & ernest proceding tooching thexecucion of his office
& that vpon his complaynt Letteres wer written vnto the
Richard a lee sirveiour of Calais to make answer vnto such
thinges as the sayd Comptroller in his said lettere (which
remayn with the Secretary Master Wryothesley) layd to
the surveiours charge / further commaunding the sayd
comptroller to stay all such fees as the superfluous
surveiours had there /

a Lettere sent to Richard a lee sirveiour of Calais to such
thinges as in a stedule inclosed in his lettere was sent vnto hym
with commaundement to vse hym self to the kinges chief officers
there as to his dewtie doth apperteyn

A Lettere vnto Thomas fowler of Calais declaring
the recept of a bill sent from hym conteynyng the
(as he thought) the extraordinary offices in Calais
and that in the Counsailles opinion he had sent a wrong
informacion vnto them and that therfor he shuld eftesones
prevse the said bill (the copy wherof whas sent vnto hym
trahed vpon sum poyntes) and thervpon vpon the sight cous
to send vnto them a perfait booke of the same thinges /

A Lettere of Justice vndre the stampe was sent to John Heron of chipchace
keper of Tyndale & Riddayesale / in the fauour of
at the sure of my lord of Norfolk /
