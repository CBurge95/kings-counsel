The xxiijth Daye of November the king
and the quene accompaignied only with the
lord privey Seale the lord admyrall
The Master of the horses & the vicechamberlayn
of his privey counsail and with the ladyes
gentlemen and gentlewomen of their privey
chambers departed to Okyng wh wheras they
laye vntil the vijth of december vpon which
Daye his highnes with the quenes grace and
the others before sayd departed to Oteland
& there remayned vntil the xviijth of the
same monethe & vpon that daye cam to hampton
courte And in this tyme of absence Sir
Thomas Wryothesley the knyght one of the two
principall Secretarys witH the comptroller
& the household remayned still at
The lordes of the Counsail beyng
to their homes owne howses and yet

shuld haue allowed vnto him the somme of xxvjs viijd by
the weke for the dyet of the Lady Lysle one prist and
three other persones and the wages of the sayd prist and
that vpon aduertisement from the sayd Fraunces of such
other necessary chardges as he hath bene at for the sayde
Ladt he shuld haue allowaunce aswell for the same as
for the nombre of wekes after the rate aforsaid

It was agreed that a lettere shuld be wrytten to sir Thomas
Wharton signifying vnto him that wheras he had committed

It was agred that the lord after long & mature
consultacion that their opinions was that the lord leonard
gray he late depute the kinges depute in Irland
being led by the affection / which he bore to the gare
tynes by reason of the mariage betwene his sistre and
the late Erle of Kyldare had done & commytted such
haynous offences agaynst the kinges Maieste and especially

in these fyve poyntes folowyng that is to say

The entreteynyng of Mcgaret oconour, omores sones
priour walsh & his brother knowyng the same to be the kinges
rebelles traytours & enemyes & haveng no that before
they had any pardon /

The setting vp of forganamyn the kinges enemye

The setting at large of Tibet fitzpiers fitzgaret

The procuryng & mayntenance of Omores sonnes

The entreteynyng of Edmund ashebold after that

his bydding him to shifte for hym self

Which forsayd articles appere nere at length in

Turlogh otole a wild irishman & an outlaw repayred hither in to England and Submitted
to thentent to submitt hym self and his brother artoge othole
vnto the kinges mercye beseching his highnes to let them
hym hold certayn landes (specified in a supplicacion remay
nyng in the custody of the forsayd Secretary) of his Maieste
by knyghtes sirvice offering hym self to be obedient to
the kinges lawes & to vse the English facion which
request semed reasonable & mete to be graunted /

It
Wheras the bishop of Dublyn made request to haue

had bene long tyme out of their handes and now
was by the kinges depute & h at his Maiestes charges

Sir Thomas Horton alias Baker prist vicar of Cawne
in the Countye of Wilteshire was sent vpp by sir Henry
Long knight as suspect to be a papist for having Thomas
becketes name in one of his bookes and being examyned befor
the sayd Counsaill and appering as far as could be
gathered to haue left the same vnput out of negligence
rather then malice was bounden in a recognisaunce of
fourty poundes as foloweth and so dismissed with a lettere
to sir Henry long to se the same fulfilled accordingly


pagebreak


Thomas Horton alias baker de villa

The Condicion of this recognisaunce is that if the
sayd Thomas Horton alias Baker do personally appere
before the kinges Justices of the peax in Wilteshir
at the next generall Sessions of the peax in
the says shire to doo and obsirue such thinges as
shalbe ther enioyned to him and alsoo doo openly
in his parishe church crye the kinges highnes
mercy knowwledging his offence and foly in not
obsiruyng the kinges highnes Iniunctions and doo
fromhensforth from tyme to tyme obsirue and kepe
the same / that then etc. /
