Att westminster the xxjth day off
Aprile being than present as the
day before

Thre seuerall letteres wer sent to my lord deputye of
Callais / Sir Jhon wallop / and the surveyour off Callais
for thapprehension of one Thomas Fissher Mason who
was thowght to be there in the kinges worckes / thesame to
be safelye sent hither

Jhon gonge mercer and one Cobbe a skolemaster
appered before the cownsell And being layed to the sayd
Cobbes charge the translation off a certeyne Postilla vpon
the gospelles, wherein wer founde sondry seditious and
erroneous opinions was therefore committed vnto warde
The sayd John gonge hauing ben the mayntenor as
entreteynor off the sayde Gonge Cobbe wythin his hows was
bonde by recognisance as ensueth for his forthcomming
att all tymes vpon a convenient warning

The condition off this recognisance is suche as if thaboue
bounden Jhon gonge do att all tymes vpon a sufficient war
ning appere before cownsell / and nott departe before suche
tyme as he shall be by any two of them dismissed than etc. or ells etc.

Jhon Bell and John raynolde being licensed to go
vnto the see for reprisalles etc. this certified the veassells
according to theyre bounde whiche they entended to vse in
thesame / that is to say iiij bootes off rye eche off
them off the burden by Estimation off xxti toone /

A lettere was written to the Custumers off the porte off london
that forasmuche as the staplers shippes now redy to departe
myght by tarrying suffer summe notable hurtt and dammage
they sholde permitt and suffer thesame to departe notwithstonding
they hadde not yett receyued no pryvye seale for theyre discharge
on that behalff whiche they sholde haue with all diligence /
