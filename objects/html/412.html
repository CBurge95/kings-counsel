A lettere was sent to Sir henry Sauell and Sir
thomas wentworth / to signifie with all diligence to
the cownsell how the skottishe prisoners wer lodged by
the way and whiche off them vsed in theyre iourney
hitherward to lye together / to thintent that theyre
lodginges might be here appoynted accordinglye /

Letteres warre redde to the cownsell directed to the kinges
maiestie from my lorde lisle and the bisshop off duresme
towching the taking off Stevin davison etc. theves off
teresdale in skotland /

Vpon an information off one off the kinges highnes
pourveyoures for waynskott / that Sir humfrey browne hauing
certeyne waynskott to sell / and beng required off the sayde
pourveyour to haue parte thereoff vpon reasonable prices for
the kinges highnes necessarie affarres / refused neverthelesse to make sale
thereoff vndre suche prices as wer estemed to be far aboue
reason / A lettere was sent to the sayde Sir humfrey aduising
thesame to sell the sayde waynskott for the pourpos abouesaide
ether at sych a price as sholde be to him appoynted by
his highnes officers, other ells at suche a price as the same
by fowre indifferent men sholde be iudged to be worth

Letteres wer redde in the cownsell directed from the
lorde deputie off Jrelande and the rest off the cownsell there /
to the kinges maiestie conteyning in effect the nombre off the
retinew there to be vc fyftye / Item the request off nel conelagh

Letteres wer receyued from Sir thomas wharton off the date off
xvjth day of december wyth aduertisementes of certeyne exploses
don xxti myle wythin skotlande by robin foster etc. The
disfurniture off the west marches by the reason off my lorde off cumbreland
departing to my lord lisle / and the rawnsoming off diuerse good prisoners
for small prices / wyth the taking allso off the laide off fentre /

This day the cownsell assembled in the sterre chawmber / and
therle off Anguisshe Casseilles The lorde off glencarne , the
lorde Somerwell the lorde Maxwell wyth all the other prisoners being presented vnto
them after a declaration made by the lorde chawncellour off
the kinges naturall clemencye and goodnes, regarding wer vpon
theyre promes to be trew prisoners , wer reparted to noble
men and gentilmen every off them according to theyre honour
and estimation
