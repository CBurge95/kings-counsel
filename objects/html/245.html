At Pontfret the iijde day of September
being present /
The Duke of Suffolk
The Lord priuey Seale
The gret Chambrelain of Englande
The gret Admiral of Englande
The Comptroller of household /
The Master of the horses
The vicechambrelain
Sir Thomas Wryothesley Secretary
The Chauncelour of Thaugmentacions

A lettere was written to syr Thomas Wharton to permitt the
pensioners being at Carlisle to departe euery to his home
putting themselfes nevertheles in ordre to be redy vpon warnyng
to syrve the king And furthermore that he shuld differr
to make redresse vpon the borders / vntil that he was aduertised
that the scottes did for their parte better iustice vpon theast
marches vsyng nevertheles such delayes as the same semed
not to procede vpon in determinacion And as touching
the chalenge made by the Armestronges against the
Graymes his highnes was contented that the same shuld
be perfourmed so as his highnes wer furst aduertised
of the circumstances therof and that it be done in an
indifferent place / before the wardens of both the marches
